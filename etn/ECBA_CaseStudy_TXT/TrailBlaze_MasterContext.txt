COMPANY BACKGROUND (available any session)

TrailBlaze Adventures is a mid-sized adventure travel company operating in the eastern US. Founded in 2018, they offer guided outdoor trips ranging from half-day hikes to week-long expeditions. Revenue is split roughly 60% weekend trips, 40% multi-day packages. The company employs 12 full-time staff and contracts approximately 40 guides on a per-trip basis.


SESSION 1 REVEAL — Company brief + symptom only
(What participants receive in the one-pager and scenario brief)

- Weekend trip bookings have declined 18% over the past two quarters
- The CEO believes the solution is a new booking platform
- The Head of Operations believes the real issue is guide availability and scheduling
- Marketing believes it is a brand awareness problem among urban younger demographics
- The Finance Director has frozen the technology budget pending a business case
- No BA has been involved yet — leadership is in solution mode

Facilitator note: Do not confirm which diagnosis is correct. The point of Session 1 is that participants must define the need before any solution is valid.


SESSION 2 REVEAL — BA engagement begins
(Drip this into the Session 2 scenario brief)

- Leadership has agreed to bring in a BA before committing budget
- Stakeholder interviews have been scheduled with: CEO, Head of Ops, Marketing Lead, Finance Director, Lead Guide Representative
- Initial interview outputs (facilitator can share as elicitation excerpts):
  - CEO: "We're losing ground to newer platforms. We need a modern booking experience."
  - Head of Ops: "We turn away 20% of booking requests because guides aren't available on short notice. That's the real problem."
  - Marketing Lead: "Our brand doesn't show up in search for 'weekend adventure trips.' We're invisible to our target market."
  - Finance Director: "We need a business case with clear ROI before any investment. I'm not approving another tech project that doesn't pay back."
  - Lead Guide: "I find out about trips 48 hours before. That's not enough time to prepare properly."
- Key insight: three different root causes are emerging — product, operations, and marketing. The BA must define scope before recommending an approach.


SESSION 3 REVEAL — Elicitation outputs + requirements emerging
(Use for requirements elicitation exercises)

- BA has completed stakeholder interviews and two workshops
- Agreed scope: focus on weekend trip booking experience + guide assignment workflow
- Top elicited needs (not yet requirements — participants convert them):
  - Customers want to browse and book trips without calling an office
  - Customers want to know guide credentials before booking
  - Operations needs 72-hour minimum notice before guide assignment is confirmed
  - Operations wants a dashboard showing all upcoming trips and guide status
  - Marketing wants trip listings to be searchable and shareable
  - Finance requires all payments to be PCI-compliant and refunds to be automated
- Constraints confirmed:
  - Team: 1 frontend, 1 backend, 1 QA, 0.5 BA/product
  - Budget approved for a 10-week MVP build (contingent on business case sign-off)
  - Existing catalog: 50 trails with contracted partner guides
  - Guide contracts are seasonal — availability varies by month


SESSION 4 REVEAL — Solution options + RTM
(Use for solution evaluation and traceability exercises)

- Three solution options have been proposed for stakeholder review:
  1. Enhance the existing website with a self-serve booking flow (low cost, 8 weeks)
  2. Build a new mobile-first booking platform with guide matching (higher cost, 14 weeks)
  3. Partner with an existing outdoor booking marketplace (no build, revenue share model)
- MVP feature set agreed after workshop (participants build RTM from this):
  - Browse and filter trips by date, difficulty, location
  - Bookable trip checkout with payment and confirmation email
  - Guide assignment dashboard (internal ops)
  - Customer itinerary PDF delivery
  - Pilot NPS survey
- NFRs confirmed:
  - Booking service availability: 99.5% during peak hours
  - Search performance: results < 500ms at 95th percentile
  - PCI-compliant payment flow; customer data encrypted at rest
  - Support 200 concurrent booking sessions during pilot
- KPIs agreed:
  - Booking conversion rate
  - 100 pilot bookings in first 30 days
  - NPS ≥ 8
  - On-time departure rate ≥ 95%
  - Payment success rate ≥ 99%
  - Guide assignment confirmed < 24 hours before trip


SESSION 5 REVEAL — Pilot results + lessons learned
(Use for BA effectiveness and continuous improvement exercises)

- Pilot ran for 30 days following MVP launch
- Results:
  - 87 bookings (target: 100) — missed by 13%
  - NPS: 8.2 (target met)
  - On-time departure rate: 91% (target: 95% — missed)
  - Payment success rate: 99.4% (target met)
  - Guide assignment < 24 hours: 78% (target not formally measured — gap identified)
- Root cause of missed targets:
  - Bookings short: search SEO not optimised at launch; marketing campaign delayed 2 weeks
  - Departure rate: ops dashboard did not surface same-day changes; guides missed updates
- Stakeholder satisfaction scores (BA effectiveness measurement):
  - CEO: 4/5 — "Good progress but we need to move faster on mobile"
  - Head of Ops: 3/5 — "Dashboard helped but guide comms still broke down"
  - Marketing Lead: 5/5 — "Finally have a platform we can promote"
  - Finance Director: 4/5 — "ROI trajectory is positive, want quarterly reviews"
- Lessons learned (participants identify gaps in BA approach and recommend next steps):
  - Guide notification workflow was elicited but not prioritised — sequencing error
  - SEO requirements were in scope but not traced to any sprint — traceability gap
  - Recommend: post-pilot BA retrospective, updated RTM, refined ops workflow requirements
