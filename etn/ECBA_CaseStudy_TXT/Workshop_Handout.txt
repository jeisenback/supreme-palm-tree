Welcome to Session 1. You don’t need prior BA experience to work through this — the exercises are designed for you to figure it out together. That’s the point.

TrailBlaze scenario
TrailBlaze Adventures is a mid-sized adventure travel company. Weekend trip bookings have declined 18% over the past two quarters. The CEO wants to build a new booking platform. The Head of Operations thinks the real problem is guide availability. Marketing believes it's a brand awareness issue. The Finance Director has frozen the technology budget pending a business case.

Your task in this session: before anyone proposes a solution, define the real problem, understand what is at stake, and identify who needs to be involved. That’s the role of a business analyst (BA) — and it’s what you’ll practice today.

Exercise 1 — BACCM Analysis (15 minutes)
The BACCM (Business Analysis Core Concept Model) is the foundational BA framework. It has six interdependent elements: Change, Need, Solution, Stakeholder, Value, and Context. No element stands alone — understanding one requires understanding its relationship to the others.

Exercise 2 — Stakeholder List (10 minutes)
Identify at least 6 stakeholders. For each, note:
- Role
- Interest level (H/M/L)
- Influence level (H/M/L)
- Their stake — what is actually at risk for them?

Rubric (12 pts total)
This is a learning rubric — it tells you what strong BA thinking looks like at this stage, not a final grade.
- BACCM chart (0–3): all 6 elements, TrailBlaze-specific, NEED does not contain a solution
- Stakeholder list (0–3): 6+ stakeholders, stake defined (not just role title)
- Problem statement (0–3): specific, measurable, no solution
- Presentation clarity (0–3): concise, BA-framed language
