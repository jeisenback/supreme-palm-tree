# BA Case Workshop — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Case-based problem solving in small groups (90 min)
**Audience:** BAs preparing for case-style interviews or assessment centers
**Goal:** Participants work through a realistic BA case, present their approach, and receive structured feedback.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Structure an ambiguous BA problem using a repeatable framework
- Ask clarifying questions that expose hidden assumptions
- Present a requirements approach with traceable rationale

## Materials

- [ ] Case packet (distributed at session start — do not share in advance)
- [ ] Case debrief guide (facilitator only)
- [ ] Whiteboard or shared digital workspace per group
- [ ] Timer

## Agenda

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| 0:00 | Welcome + case overview | 5 min |
| 0:05 | Clarifying questions round (open to facilitator) | 10 min |
| 0:15 | Small group case work | 25 min |
| 0:40 | Group presentations (5 min each, 3–4 groups) | 20 min |
| 1:00 | Facilitator debrief: what hiring managers look for | 15 min |
| 1:15 | Wrap-up + take-home challenge | 5 min |

## The Case Format

Each case presents:
1. **Situation brief** — company, industry, problem statement (1 paragraph)
2. **Stakeholder cast** — 3–5 named stakeholders with partial context
3. **Deliverable ask** — "Define your approach to requirements gathering for..."
4. **Constraint** — time, budget, or organizational friction

[FACILITATOR: Select a case appropriate for the group's experience level. Beginner cases have clear stakeholders and a single domain. Advanced cases have conflicting stakeholders and cross-functional scope.]

## Facilitation Notes

### Clarifying Questions Round (10 min)
[FACILITATOR: This is a critical skill test. Do NOT answer everything — model what a hiring manager does: answer some questions, deflect others ("use your judgment"), and reward incisive questions with positive acknowledgment ("great question — here's what I can tell you...")]

Watch for participants who ask no questions — in a real case interview, silence signals passivity.

### Case Work (25 min)
[FACILITATOR: Circulate between groups. Listen for reasoning, not just answers. The process matters more than the output. Listen for: "We assumed... because..." and "We considered X but rejected it because..."]

Coaching prompts if groups are stuck:
- "What would you need to know before recommending anything?"
- "Who has the most to lose if this goes wrong?"
- "What's the fastest way to get signal on the highest-risk assumption?"

### Presentations (20 min)
[FACILITATOR: Time groups to 5 min each. Stop them if they run over — this simulates real constraints. Ask one follow-up question per group.]

Good follow-up questions:
- "Why that approach vs. a different technique?"
- "What would change your recommendation?"
- "How would you handle pushback from [named stakeholder]?"

### Facilitator Debrief (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Walk through what distinguishes strong from weak case responses:
- Strong: structured thinking, explicit tradeoffs, traceable rationale
- Weak: jumping to solutions, ignoring stakeholders, no validation plan

Normalize that the "right answer" is less important than demonstrating structured BA thinking.]

## Debrief Questions

- What clarifying question do you wish you'd asked?
- How did your group's approach differ from others? What drove that?
- What would you do differently in a real case interview?
