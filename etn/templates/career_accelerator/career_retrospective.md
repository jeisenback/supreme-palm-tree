# Career Retrospective — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Reflective workshop using agile retrospective format (75 min)
**Audience:** Mid-career BAs reassessing direction; new members doing a first career review
**Goal:** Participants generate an honest picture of their career so far and draft a focused direction statement.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Identify patterns in their career: what energized vs. drained them
- Articulate their "BA superpower" — the one thing they do better than most
- Draft a one-sentence career direction statement for the next 12–18 months

## Materials

- [ ] Retrospective worksheet (participant_handout.md)
- [ ] Sticky notes (physical or Miro)
- [ ] Energy timeline template (a simple line graph — provided in worksheet)

## Agenda

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| 0:00 | Welcome + setting the container | 5 min |
| 0:05 | Energy timeline: plot your career highs and lows | 15 min |
| 0:20 | Small group share: what patterns show up? | 10 min |
| 0:30 | What went well / what drained you / what I'd do differently | 15 min |
| 0:45 | Superpower extraction: what do you do better than most? | 10 min |
| 0:55 | Direction statement draft | 10 min |
| 1:05 | Partner share + refine | 5 min |
| 1:10 | Wrap-up + voluntary shares | 5 min |

## Facilitation Notes

### Setting the Container (5 min)
[FACILITATOR: This session asks people to reflect honestly on regrets and wrong turns. Create psychological safety explicitly: "What we say here stays here. The point is clarity, not judgment."]

### Energy Timeline (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Ask participants to draw a freehand line graph: x-axis = career timeline, y-axis = energy/engagement level. Mark key moments (first BA role, a project they loved, a role they dreaded). Silence is OK — this is reflective work.]

Don't rush. People who look stuck are usually processing.

### What Went Well / What Drained / What I'd Do Differently (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Classic retro format adapted for career. Three sticky note columns. Push for specificity — not "bad manager" but "decision-making opacity made me feel powerless."]

Watch for participants who only write positives — gently probe: "Is there anything you'd do differently, even one small thing?"

### Superpower Extraction (10 min)
[FACILITATOR: Ask the inverting question: "What do colleagues come to you for that they don't go to others?" This often surfaces the superpower better than direct self-assessment. Common BA superpowers: cutting through ambiguity, reading stakeholder dynamics, turning chaos into structure, translating tech to business.]

### Direction Statement (10 min)
Format: "In the next [12–18 months], I want to [do more/less/different] because [what matters to me now]."

[FACILITATOR: This is a direction, not a commitment. Emphasize: it can change. The value is clarity today.]

## Debrief Questions

- What surprised you about your own energy timeline?
- What's the difference between what you're good at and what gives you energy?
- What would you tell a new BA starting their first role?
