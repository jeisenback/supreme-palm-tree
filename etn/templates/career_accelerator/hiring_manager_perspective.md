# Hiring Manager Perspective — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Fireside chat + Q&A with 1–2 hiring managers (60 min)
**Audience:** Job-seeking BAs at any level
**Goal:** Participants understand what hiring managers actually look for (vs. what job postings say) and leave with 2–3 concrete changes to their job search approach.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Describe the gap between job posting language and hiring decision criteria
- Identify red flags and green flags that hiring managers notice in interviews
- Ask questions in interviews that demonstrate strategic BA thinking

## Guest Profile

Ideal guests: hiring managers who have interviewed and hired BAs within the last 12 months. Aim for one person who hires entry-level/ECBA-level BAs and one who hires mid-senior.

[FACILITATOR: Pre-interview guests with these questions before the session so you're not surprised by answers. Ask: "What's the thing you look for that never appears in a job description?"]

## Moderator Question Bank

### What actually matters
- "Walk us through the last BA you hired. What tipped the decision?"
- "What's on a resume that immediately signals 'yes, interview this person'?"
- "What's on a resume that makes you hesitate — even if the experience looks right?"

### The interview itself
- "What question do you ask in every BA interview? What are you listening for?"
- "What answer signals a candidate is just reciting vs. actually thinking?"
- "What's a red flag that doesn't show up until the interview?"

### What candidates get wrong
- "What do most BA candidates underestimate about the role?"
- "If you could give every BA candidate one piece of advice before they walk in, what would it be?"

### Surprise questions
- "Has a candidate ever impressed you with a question they asked YOU? What was it?"
- "What's something that looked like a weakness on paper but turned out to be a strength?"

## Facilitation Notes

### Opening (5 min)
[FACILITATOR: Frame this as a rare chance to hear the unfiltered version — not HR language, not job posting language. "We're going to hear what the hiring decision actually looks like from the inside."]

### Managing Candor
[FACILITATOR: Hiring managers will often soften answers in public settings. Give permission to be direct: "We're a safe audience — you can say the thing you usually can't say in an interview."]

If a guest is being too polished, try: "If you could only ask ONE question in a 30-minute interview, what would it be?"

### Audience Q&A (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Curate written questions submitted before the Q&A. Prioritize: questions about the gap between posting and reality, questions about specific signals (portfolio, certifications, ECBA), and questions from entry-level attendees who feel most uncertain.]

## Post-Session Follow-Up

[FACILITATOR: If guests are willing, offer an optional 10-min networking break. Some of the best connections happen in the 5 minutes after the session.]
