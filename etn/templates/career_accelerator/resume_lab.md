# Resume Lab — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Hands-on workshop with peer review (90 min)
**Audience:** Job-seeking BAs at any level
**Goal:** Participants leave with a revised, BA-specific resume section and two pieces of peer feedback.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Reframe experience bullets using BA impact language (outcomes, not activities)
- Identify which BABOK knowledge areas their experience demonstrates
- Give and receive structured resume feedback

## Facilitation Notes

### What Makes a BA Resume Different (10 min)
[FACILITATOR: The core insight is that BAs are often invisible in outcomes. The work is to make the BA's contribution explicit: "I facilitated the workshops that produced the requirements that enabled the system that saved $X."]

Show two versions of the same bullet:
- **Before:** "Conducted stakeholder interviews"
- **After:** "Facilitated 12 stakeholder interviews across 4 departments, synthesizing conflicting requirements into a signed-off BRD that unblocked a 6-month delivery stall"

### Impact Reframe Activity (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Walk through the formula: Action verb + BA-specific method + quantified outcome + business impact. Not every bullet needs all four, but push for at least two.]

Common traps:
- "Responsible for..." (passive, no ownership)
- "Helped with..." (undersells contribution)
- "Worked on..." (says nothing)

### Hands-On Rewrite (20 min)
[FACILITATOR: Have participants pick ONE bullet to rewrite — not their whole resume. Perfection is the enemy here. One great bullet demonstrates the skill.]

Circulate and coach. If someone is stuck, ask: "What would have happened if YOU hadn't done this work?"

### Peer Review (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Use the structured form — don't let this become vague encouragement. The three questions are: (1) What's the strongest bullet and why? (2) Which bullet is hardest to understand? (3) What question does this resume NOT answer that a hiring manager would have?]

## Debrief Questions

- What was the hardest part of rewriting your own experience?
- What patterns did you notice in the peer feedback?
- What's one thing you'll change before your next application?
