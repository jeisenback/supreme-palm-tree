# Mock Interview Workshop — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Paired mock interviews with structured debrief (90 min)
**Audience:** BAs preparing for job interviews
**Goal:** Participants complete at least one full mock interview and receive structured feedback on content and delivery.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Structure a BA behavioral answer using STAR format with BA-specific language
- Identify their strongest 3 "go-to" stories and which question types they address
- Demonstrate one concrete improvement from peer feedback

## Materials

- [ ] Question bank (BA behavioral + technical questions, 20+)
- [ ] STAR coaching card (participant_handout.md)
- [ ] Feedback form for interviewers
- [ ] Timer (phone or shared screen)

## Facilitation Notes

### STAR Refresher + BA Framing (10 min)
[FACILITATOR: Standard STAR is fine, but BAs need to make their analytical contribution explicit. Add an "A²" layer: Action (what you did) → Analysis (how you reasoned). "I gathered requirements" is weak. "I structured elicitation to surface latent needs the team didn't know to ask for" shows BA thinking.]

The four most common BA interview question types:
1. Elicitation ("Tell me about a time you had to get requirements from reluctant stakeholders")
2. Conflict ("Describe a situation where stakeholders disagreed on requirements")
3. Ambiguity ("Walk me through how you handle unclear or changing scope")
4. Delivery ("Tell me about a project where BA work directly impacted the outcome")

### Story Inventory (10 min)
[FACILITATOR: Have participants write down 3 stories (2–3 sentences each) that they can adapt. The goal is to stop improvising in interviews. Each story should be mappable to at least 2 question types.]

### Running the Mock Interview (15 min each)
[FACILITATOR: Interviewers should pick 2 questions from the bank — one behavioral, one technical or situational. Don't coach during the interview. Take notes on the feedback form.]

Common delivery issues to flag:
- Too long (>3 min per answer)
- Jumping to solution before describing situation
- "We" without clarifying the individual's contribution
- Not landing the impact ("and then the project finished")

### Full Group Debrief (15 min)
[FACILITATOR: Ask for patterns, not individuals. "What's one delivery habit you want to improve?" rather than calling out specific people.]

## Debrief Questions

- What felt different answering with STAR structure vs. without?
- Which question type is hardest for you and why?
- What story are you most confident in now? What made it land?
