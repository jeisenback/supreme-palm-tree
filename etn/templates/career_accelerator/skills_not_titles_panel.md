# Skills Not Titles Panel — Facilitator Guide

## Session Overview

**Format:** Moderated panel + Q&A (75 min)
**Audience:** BAs exploring adjacent roles and career paths
**Goal:** Panelists illuminate non-obvious career paths where BA skills transfer; participants leave with 1–2 specific new paths to research.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:
- Name 3+ roles where BA skills transfer without a title match
- Identify which of their skills are most transferable to adjacent domains
- Articulate a targeted question they'd ask in an informational interview

## Panel Composition (recommended)

3–4 panelists spanning diverse paths. Suggested mix:
- BA who transitioned to Product Management
- BA in a non-tech domain (healthcare, government, nonprofit)
- BA who moved into consulting or independent work
- Senior BA / BA manager on what they wish they'd known earlier

[FACILITATOR: Prioritize panelists who can speak to skills explicitly, not just title progression. Ask them in prep: "What BA skills do you use that your job description doesn't mention?"]

| 1:05 | Lightning round: "One thing you'd tell your BA-student self" | 5 min |
| 1:10 | Wrap-up + networking prompt | 5 min |

## Moderator Question Bank

### Warm-up
- "What's the most surprising place your BA skills have shown up in your current role?"

### Skills transfer
- "What specific techniques from BA practice do you use that people in your role typically don't have?"
- "What was the hardest skill to translate — and what did that translation look like?"

### Career path
- "What role did you take that looked like a step sideways but turned out to be a career accelerator?"
- "What would you look for in a BA's background if you were hiring for your current role?"

### Audience-facing
- "What question should someone ask in an informational interview that most people don't?"

## Facilitation Notes

### Framing the Lens (5 min)
[FACILITATOR: Establish that job titles are lagging indicators of skills. The BA title is 20 years old; the skills are timeless. Today's conversation is about skills — not career ladders, not titles, not org charts.]

### Keeping It Concrete
[FACILITATOR: Push back on abstract answers. "Stakeholder communication" is not specific enough. Push for: "In my role as X, I use elicitation when I need to..." or "The technique that transferred was...".]

### Audience Q&A (20 min)
[FACILITATOR: Collect written questions on index cards or via slido at the start. Curate before the Q&A segment — surface the 4–5 most common themes, not the most wordy questions.]

## Post-Panel Networking Prompt

[FACILITATOR: Close with a structured prompt: "Find one panelist or audience member whose path you want to know more about. Introduce yourself with one thing you're working on right now."]
