## Opening Question (10 min)

*Ask all panelists — go around the table*

"I'd like to start with a question for everyone. [OPENING QUESTION — e.g., 'What's the most important thing a BA can do in the first 30 days of a new engagement?']

Let's go left to right. [FIRST PANELIST NAME], let's start with you."

[MODERATOR: Time each answer to 90 seconds. Gently interrupt with "Let me pause you there and come back to that — " if needed. After the round, note the most interesting tension or theme: "I notice [PANELIST A] and [PANELIST B] came at that differently — we'll come back to that."]

## Audience Q&A (20 min)

"Now I'd like to open it up to your questions. [METHOD: Please raise your hand / Submit via Slido at [URL] / Write on your card and pass it forward]."

[MODERATOR: Read curated questions — not every question, not in order received. Prioritize: specific over vague, questions that play to panelist expertise, questions you haven't already covered. Attribute: "This question comes from [FIRST NAME/ROLE if willing]."]

If questions dry up:
"While we wait, I have one I've been holding: [BACKUP QUESTION]."

---

## Lightning Round (5 min)

*Ask all panelists — keep answers to 15 seconds*

"I want to end with a lightning round. One short answer each. [LIGHTNING QUESTION — e.g., 'The one piece of advice you'd give your earlier self.']"

[MODERATOR: Go in reverse intro order to give the last panelist a different spotlight moment.]

## Moderator Question Bank

*Use these as-is or adapt for your panel's topic.*

### On the BA role
- "What does a great day look like in your role? What does a bad one look like?"
- "What does your title NOT tell people about what you actually do?"
- "What skill that's not in your job description has been most valuable?"

### On career paths
- "What role did you take that looked like a lateral move but wasn't?"
- "What would you look for in a BA candidate that most people don't think to demonstrate?"

### On the field
- "What's changing about what BAs need to know in the next 3 years?"
- "What tool or technique do you think is overrated? Underrated?"

### For wrap-up
- "What's a question you wish someone had asked tonight?"
- "What's one thing you want this audience to remember when they leave?"
