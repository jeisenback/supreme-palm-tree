## Format Selection Guide

### Choose v1 (Monthly + B) when:
- Participants are working professionals who need a relaxed pace.
- The chapter has an established SME network with 5+ available speakers.
- Study group momentum is a concern — monthly sessions are sustainable for most adult learners.
- You have 5 months to run the series.

### Choose v2 (Bi-Weekly + B) when:
- The cohort has a specific exam date within 10–12 weeks.
- Participants can reliably commit 3+ hours per week.
- You can confirm 5 SME speakers before the series starts.
- Group cohesion is strong — weekly contact keeps energy high.

### Choose v3 (Bi-Weekly, A Only) when:
- SME recruitment is uncertain or the chapter has no established speaker pipeline.
- Participants want a focused, lower-commitment format (2.5 hrs/week vs. 3 hrs/week).
- You are running the series for the first time and want simpler logistics.
- Participants are self-motivated enough to engage seriously with the homework challenge tasks.

### Choose v4 (Bi-Weekly, Combined) when:
- One strong practitioner is available for the full 10 weeks.
- Participants prefer a single 2-hour meeting over two separate shorter meetings.
- The facilitator has BA field experience and can run the B segment if the SME is unavailable.
- You want the full A+B experience with minimal scheduling complexity.

## BABOK Reading Expectation (All Formats)

Participants are expected to arrive at each Session A having read the required chapters. The following is honest guidance on what that means.

| Chapter Set | Approximate Pages | Realistic Reading Time |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| Ch. 1 + Ch. 2 §2.1–2.4 | ~35 pages | 2–2.5 hrs with notes |
| Ch. 3 + Ch. 4 | ~70 pages | 3–4 hrs with notes |
| Ch. 6 + Ch. 8 | ~65 pages | 3–3.5 hrs with notes |
| Ch. 7 + Ch. 9 | ~60 pages | 2.5–3 hrs with notes |
| Ch. 10 (selected) + review | ~40 pages + review | 2–3 hrs |

**Facilitator note:** Not all participants will complete the reading before every session. This is a known risk, not an anomaly. Design the concept instruction phases to stand alone — use the BABOK as a reference and anchor, not as a prerequisite for comprehension. Participants who did not read can still participate; participants who read will go deeper in the exercises.
