## Overview

This is the **bi-weekly + Session B** delivery format. All five A sessions and all five B sessions run, completing in approximately **10 weeks**. A sessions are spaced 2 weeks apart. Each B session runs 1 week after the preceding A session.

The content, exercises, artifacts, and ECBA domain coverage are identical to the base series. What changes is the time participants have between sessions. Read every note in this plan through that lens: the pedagogical design is unchanged; the pacing demand on participants is higher.

## Reading Schedule

Participants have 2 weeks per chapter set — not 4. Communicate this at Session 0 and again at each Session A close.

| Session A | Required Reading | Reading Window |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1A | BABOK Ch. 1 (Introduction) + Ch. 2 §2.1–2.4 (Key Concepts) | Before Week 1 |
| 2A | BABOK Ch. 3 (BA Planning & Monitoring) + Ch. 4 (Elicitation & Collaboration) | Weeks 1–2 |
| 3A | BABOK Ch. 6 (Requirements Lifecycle Management) + Ch. 8 (Strategy Analysis) | Weeks 3–4 |
| 4A | BABOK Ch. 7 (RADD) + Ch. 9 (Solution Evaluation) | Weeks 5–6 |
| 5A | BABOK Ch. 10 (Techniques) + review all personal notes from Sessions 1–4 | Weeks 7–8 |

**Facilitator note:** At the close of each Session A, name the reading for the next A session explicitly. Participants have only 2 weeks. A generic "read before next session" instruction is not enough — tell them which chapters, how many pages, and what to watch for as they read.

**Suggested reading approach for participants:** Two 30–45 minute sessions per week, on separate days. First session: read and take Cornell notes. Second session: close the book and write a one-paragraph summary from memory before reviewing your notes. This approach takes 60–90 minutes per week and replaces the need for a longer single-sitting review.

## Homework Calibration

The homework deliverables are the same as v1 (base series). The written artifact should be scoped toward quality over length — a tight, well-reasoned paragraph is acceptable when the window is 1 week, not 3. Set this expectation at Session 0 and repeat it at the close of each Session A when assigning homework.

| After Session | Written Artifact | MCQ Count |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1A | Refined problem statement + 1-paragraph value statement | 6 |
| 2A | One-page BA approach recommendation (approach, stakeholders, rationale) | 6 |
| 3A | 3 raw needs converted to written requirements | 6 |
| 4A | 1-paragraph solution recommendation + RTM stub (3 requirements traced) | 6 |
| 5A | None — review all previous MCQs; prepare stretch question response from 4B | None |

## Session B Structure (All Sessions)

Session B runs approximately 1 week after each A session. Participants arrive with:
- Their in-session artifact from Session A (draft state)
- Partial homework progress (not complete — 1 week is a short window)
- Questions from their first attempt at applying the concept

The SME should expect raw, in-progress work. The friction the SME introduces should calibrate to this — probe the reasoning, not the polish.

Session B is 60 minutes:

| Phase | Activity | Time |
|-------|----------|------|
| 1 — Setup | Facilitator recaps Session A artifact work (2–3 sentences); introduces SME | 5 min |
| 2 — Deep dive | SME-led exercise (see base plan for session-specific format) | 35 min |
| 3 — Debrief | SME steps out of character; open reflection on real-world friction | 12 min |
| 4 — Stretch + close | Stretch question for next Session A share-out; SME contact shared | 8 min |

Refer to `etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/Facilitator/Session_B_SME_Prep_Guide.md` for the full SME briefing template.

## Participant Expectations Checklist

Communicate all of the following before or at Session 0:

- [ ] This series covers 5 ECBA domains across 10 sessions in 10 weeks.
- [ ] Sessions A (90 min) and B (60 min) alternate each week.
- [ ] BABOK reading is required before each Session A — approximately 30–50 pages per 2-week window.
- [ ] Homework after each Session A includes a short written artifact and 6 exam-style multiple choice questions.
- [ ] Missing a session affects subsequent sessions — the case study builds session-to-session.
- [ ] A makeup approach: if you miss a session, review the session slides and the homework prompt before the next one. You will not receive the missed gated reveal in advance.
