# ECBA Case Study — Session 0 Onboarding Handout (v2: Bi-Weekly, With Session B)

*Distribute before Session 1A. No meeting required.*

---

## What This Series Is

This is a 10-session, 10-week ECBA exam preparation series built around one evolving case study: **TrailBlaze Adventures**, a mid-sized adventure travel company working through a BA engagement from first contact through pilot results.

Every two sessions (one A + one B) covers a set of ECBA exam domains and the corresponding BABOK knowledge areas. You will build real BA artifacts across all five session cycles and apply them to live case data that is released progressively — you will not see the full TrailBlaze story until you earn it, one session at a time.

---

## The Schedule

Sessions alternate each week. Session A (90 minutes) introduces concepts and produces artifacts. Session B (60 minutes) runs the following week with a subject matter expert who adds real-world friction to your Session A work.

| Week | Session | Length | Focus |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| 1 | 1A | 90 min | Foundations: BACCM, BA mindset, stakeholder identification, problem statement |
| 2 | 1B | 60 min | Live BACCM workshop with SME |
| 3 | 2A | 90 min | Planning, elicitation, BA approach |
| 4 | 2B | 60 min | Mock stakeholder interview with SME |
| 5 | 3A | 90 min | Change, need, requirements lifecycle |
| 6 | 3B | 60 min | Requirements conversion workshop with SME |
| 7 | 4A | 90 min | Solution analysis, RADD, RTM |
| 8 | 4B | 60 min | Solution challenge with SME |
| 9 | 5A | 90 min | Value, pilot analysis, integrated exam review |
| 10 | 5B | 60 min | Post-pilot retrospective with SME |

Total time commitment (sessions only): ~12.5 hours over 10 weeks.

## What You Need to Do Before Each Session

### Before Session 1A
Read BABOK Guide V3, Chapter 1 (Introduction) and Chapter 2, Sections 2.1–2.4. Take notes as you read — you will apply these concepts in session.

### Reading windows for subsequent sessions
Each Session A requires a BABOK chapter set read in **2 weeks**. This is roughly 30–50 pages per cycle.

| Session A | Read Before It |
|-----------|---------------|
| 2A | BABOK Ch. 3 + Ch. 4 |
| 3A | BABOK Ch. 6 + Ch. 8 |
| 4A | BABOK Ch. 7 + Ch. 9 |
| 5A | BABOK Ch. 10 + review all personal notes |

**Suggested approach:** Two reading sessions per week, 30–45 minutes each, on separate days. First session: read and take Cornell notes. Second session: close the book and write a one-paragraph summary from memory, then check your notes.

---

## Homework

Each Session A ends with a homework assignment: one short written artifact and 6 ECBA exam-style multiple choice questions. You have approximately **1 week** to complete it — before the Session B that follows.

The written artifacts are short and specific. A tight, well-reasoned paragraph is more useful than two pages of filler. The MCQs mirror exam format and difficulty. Do them individually before checking the answers.

---

## How the Case Study Works

TrailBlaze Adventures is revealed in stages. Each session unlocks a new layer of the case. You will not receive future reveals in advance — what you know at any given session is what a real BA would know at that stage of the engagement.

Your decisions in Session 1 follow you into Session 3. A problem statement without measurable criteria in Session 1 leaves you with nothing to trace in Session 3. Build with that in mind.

---

## Active Learning Environment

You will be asked to apply concepts in every session — not just hear them. Come prepared to:
- Work on exercises in groups (10–25 minutes)
- Share your work with the room
- Answer checkpoint questions mid-concept
- Sit a timed multiple choice practice round at the end of each A session

This is exam preparation. Passive attendance does not prepare you for a scenario-based exam. If you come and listen, you will leave knowing the vocabulary. If you come and work, you will leave knowing how to use it.
