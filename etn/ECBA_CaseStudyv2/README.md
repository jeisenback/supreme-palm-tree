# ECBA Case Study — Format v2: Bi-Weekly, With Session B

--- DOCUMENT GUIDE ---
What this is:   Format variant of the TrailBlaze Adventures ECBA Case Study series. Describes how to run the 5-session program on a bi-weekly schedule with all Session B SME deep dives included.
Who it's for:   Facilitator; program coordinator; board approver.
When to use it: When the cohort has the availability and urgency to complete the series in ~10 weeks instead of 5 months. Best suited for cohorts in active exam preparation.
Relationship:   Content is identical to the base series (etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/). Only schedule, pacing, and participant expectations change.
Restricted:     No — shareable with board and interested participants.
--- END GUIDE ---

## What Changes in This Format

| Factor | Base Series (v1 — Monthly) | This Format (v2 — Bi-Weekly + B) |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Cadence | A sessions monthly (4 weeks apart); B sessions mid-month | A sessions every 2 weeks; B sessions the week after each A |
| Overall duration | ~5 months (April–August) | ~10 weeks |
| Total sessions | 10 (5A + 5B) | 10 (5A + 5B) |
| Time between A sessions | 4 weeks | 2 weeks |
| Time to complete BABOK reading | 4 weeks per chapter set | 2 weeks per chapter set |
| Homework load | 6 MCQs + 1 written artifact per cycle | 6 MCQs + 1 written artifact per cycle (same volume, shorter window) |
| Session B gap after A | ~2 weeks | ~1 week |
| Who it fits | Working professionals with light exam urgency | Dedicated exam candidates with 2–3 hours/week available |

## What Does Not Change

- TrailBlaze Adventures case study, session-gated reveals, and all in-session exercises are identical.
- All artifacts (BACCM chart, stakeholder map, requirements set, RTM, solution recommendation) are the same.
- Session A is 90 minutes. Session B is 60 minutes.
- ECBA domain coverage and BABOK chapter mapping are unchanged.
- All facilitator guides, SME prep docs, and templates in `etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/` apply directly.

## When to Use This Format

Use v2 when:
- The cohort has an exam date within 3–4 months.
- Participants can reliably commit 2–3 hours per week for reading and homework.
- You have 5 SMEs available and can confirm their availability over a 10-week window.
- You are running the series in a university semester, corporate learning sprint, or similar structured time block.

Do not use v2 when:
- Participants are working full-time with unpredictable schedules — the reading pace is demanding.
- SME recruitment is uncertain — Session B depends on confirmed speakers 2–3 weeks out, not 6–8 weeks out.

## Key Facilitator Adjustments

**Reading schedule:** Participants must read each BABOK chapter set in 2 weeks, not 4. Make this explicit in the Session 0 handout and again at the close of each Session A. Suggest 30–45 minutes of reading on two separate days per week rather than one long sitting.

**Homework:** Same deliverables as v1, but the written artifact should be scoped down if needed — a strong paragraph over a weak page. Quality matters more than length when the window is compressed.

**SME booking:** Confirm each Session B speaker at least 3 weeks before their session. Because the schedule compresses, you will be recruiting Session 3B while running Session 2A. Keep a rolling 3-session booking horizon.

**Session B timing:** In v2, Session B lands approximately 1 week after Session A. This means participants bring their first pass at homework, not a refined version. The SME brief (`Facilitator/Session_B_SME_Prep_Guide.md`) should note this — the SME should expect rawer work and adjust friction accordingly.

**Stretch question to next Session A:** The gap between Session B and the following Session A is approximately 1 week. Remind participants at Session B close that the stretch question feeds the next share-out, and that the window is short.
