# INTRODUCTION

**Purpose:** The Business Plan has been developed by the IIBA Mentoring Team in order to provide Chapters with a disciplined approach to Business Planning. Chapters are free to adjust the template in any way they see fit for their Chapter, region or language. They are also free to use any other template of their own. 

**Inputs:** Chapter’s Strategic Plan, Chapter’s Bylaws, and [IIBA Strategic Plan](https://www.iiba.org/about-iiba/strategic-plan/#:~:text=In%202015%2C%20IIBA%20launched%20a,streamline%20our%20systems%20and%20processes.).

**Frequency:** Generated annually to allow for adjustments or alignment to changing market conditions and new board members. Since the Business Plan provides a foundation for the marketing and financial plans, it should be reviewed prior to those planning sessions.

**Participants:** The entire Board of Directors and program leads for the Chapter. Each Board role should provide details for their contributions in the Business Plan.

**Publication:** The Business Plan should be available to all Chapter members in good standing but is typically summarized in Chapter communications with a link to the actual document provided. 

**Relationships:** The Strategic Plan is used as an input to the Annual Business and Financial Plans. 

The Business Plan has been developed by the East Tennessee Chapter in order to provide a disciplined approach to the management of the Chapter over the calendar year. For the East Tennessee Chapter, the Business Plan will be used to align board members, volunteers and partners with the goals of the organization and commit to the Chapter’s Mission and Goals through specific and measurable objectives. The Financial Plan should be reviewed against the Business Plan prior to committing finances for the fiscal year.

### Strategic Plan vs. Business Plan Objectives

**Element**| **Strategic Plan**| **Business Plan**  
---|---|---  
**Purpose**|  Define 3-year chapter-wide outcomes aligned to vision and mission| Define annual, role-specific goals that drive progress toward strategic outcomes  
**Timeframe**|  Multi-year (2025–2027)| One year (e.g., 2025)  
**Scope**|  Chapter-level goals across all focus areas| Individual board member or team responsibilities  
**Structure**|  Strategic Objectives (e.g., O1–O9), Strategies (S1–S9), Tactics| SMART Objectives aligned to strategies or tactics  
**Measurement**|  KPIs assigned to each Strategic Objective| Deliverables, timelines, and indicators of completion  
**Ownership**|  Shared by the entire board| Assigned to specific board roles, but may be shared between multiple roles.  
**Examples**|  “Improve certification readiness and success rates among chapter members”| “Develop and launch a pilot certification prep program leveraging curated training content and peer-led study groups by Q4.”
