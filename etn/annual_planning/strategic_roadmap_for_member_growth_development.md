**IIBA East Tennessee Chapter**

**Strategic Roadmap for Member Growth & Development**

**2025 – 2026**

**Objective**

To develop a structured, tiered approach to member engagement and growth, ensuring that the IIBA East Tennessee Chapter serves as a vital resource for business analysts at every stage of their professional journey. This strategy prioritizes **accessibility, mentorship, practical skill development, and industry recognition** while creating a **clear path forward for professional advancement and certification.**

**1\. Priority Focus: Existing Members**

**(Quarter 2 & 3 of 2025)**

**Target Audience**

  * Business Analysts with multiple years of experience
  * Professionals currently working in analyst roles (but not yet certified)
  * Potential mentors for early-career professionals and students



**Goals & Initiatives**

✅ **Strengthen Community of Experienced Analysts**

  * Develop an active mentorship network where seasoned professionals can **guide early-career analysts and aspiring business analysts**.



✅ **Alternative Educational Path to Certification**

  * Create structured learning tracks to support members seeking **CBAP (Certified Business Analysis Professional), CPOA (Certificate in Product Ownership Analysis), or CBDA (Certified Business Data Analyst) certification**.
  * Offer **virtual study groups, expert-led workshops, and resource libraries** to make certification more accessible.
  * **Less than 40% of current members are certified** —by increasing certification rates, we strengthen our chapter’s credibility and create a pathway for future members.



✅ **Career Pathing & Professional Development**

  * Offer career mapping workshops to help experienced analysts **transition into leadership roles or specialized BA career paths**.
  * Provide guidance on **salary negotiations, upskilling, and executive communication strategies**.



**Implementation Timeline**

📌 **Q2 2025** – Establish mentorship network & certification education framework  
📌 **Q3 2025** – Launch study groups & structured career development sessions

**2\. Secondary Focus: Early Professionals**

**(Quarter 3 & 4 of 2025)**

**Target Audience**

  * Junior Analysts (0–3 years of experience)
  * Professionals in adjacent roles looking to **transition into business analysis**



**Goals & Initiatives**

✅ **Mentorship & Hands-On Learning**

  * **Pair early professionals with experienced BA mentors** to provide real-world guidance, feedback, and skill development.
  * Organize **live case study workshops and panel discussions** featuring experienced business analysts.



✅ **Alternative Educational Path to ECBA Certification**

  * Develop an **entry-level certification path (ECBA – Entry Certificate in Business Analysis)**.
  * Provide hands-on **project-based learning experiences** to make certification preparation more engaging.



✅ **Create Unique Career-Building Opportunities**

  * Offer **internship-like projects, real-world business case analysis, and portfolio-building exercises**.
  * Host **resume workshops, mock interviews, and networking events** to help early professionals secure analyst roles.



**Implementation Timeline**

📌 **Q3 2025** – Launch mentorship program & introduce ECBA study groups  
📌 **Q4 2025** – Begin experiential learning programs & networking initiatives

**3\. Third Focus: College Graduates**

**(Quarter 4 of 2025)**

**Target Audience**

  * Recent college graduates with an **interest in business analysis**
  * Graduates who are **unsure how to transition into an analyst role**



**Goals & Initiatives**

✅ **Bridge the Gap Between Education & Industry**

  * Offer **guidance on how to transition from academic learning to real-world business analysis**.
  * Connect graduates with **early professionals for peer mentorship & networking**.



✅ **Alternative Pathway to ECBA Certification**

  * Help recent graduates **prepare for and attain ECBA certification** through study groups and **project-based learning**.



✅ **Provide Practical Industry Exposure**

  * Develop **" BA Bootcamp" experiences** where graduates work on real business problems alongside experienced professionals.
  * Partner with local companies to provide **short-term consulting projects** or shadowing opportunities.



**Implementation Timeline**

📌 **Q4 2025** – Pair college graduates with early professionals for mentorship & offer ECBA prep resources

**4\. Last Focus: 865Academy Students**

**(Quarter 1 of 2026)**

**Target Audience**

  * High school or early college students in **865Academy** programs
  * Students with an interest in **business, technology, or problem-solving**



**Goals & Initiatives**

✅ **Introduce Business Analysis as a Career Path**

  * Host **introductory workshops** to teach students about business analysis roles and career opportunities.
  * Bring in **guest speakers from local industries** to showcase how BA professionals impact businesses.



✅ **Leverage Peer & Professional Mentorship**

  * Encourage **early professionals and college graduates** to mentor 865Academy students, **helping them understand business analysis as a potential career**.



✅ **Create an Accessible Learning Pathway**

  * Develop an **educational track that introduces students to the fundamentals of business analysis** , preparing them for potential ECBA certification down the line.
  * Provide **interactive learning experiences, such as business simulation games and case study exercises**.



**Implementation Timeline**

📌 **Q1 2026** – Launch introductory workshops & mentorship connections
