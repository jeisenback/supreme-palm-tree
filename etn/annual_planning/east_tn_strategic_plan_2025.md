# GLOSSARY

**Term** | **Definition**  
---|---  
**Formative Evaluation**|  Formative Evaluation shows what **_has, or has not been completed to date_**. This type of evaluation can be undertaken at regular intervals as a form of developmental review. **Frequency/Criteria/Target** How often, and against which criteria, for whom, and with respect to which, if any, goals and policies will the initial evaluation occur? Will there be an initial evaluation only, or a schedule of periodic evaluations? **Goals/Objectives** What goals and objectives, as defined in this strategic plan, are progressing, and how well is the team achieving the goals at the point of evaluation? **Information Usage** What will you do with the information you gather during the formative evaluation?**Move Forward Assessment** What now needs to be done, what has to happen in the future, and what should you desist from doing at this stage? **Alignment** To what extent does your progress so far align with the vision, mission and values espoused in the strategic plan? **Note:** Some chapters choose to insert a copy of a scorecard they create and maintain to validate their 3-year vision is being met instead of writing out the above detail. **Mission**|  Mission is the series of steps that guide a business to carrying out its vision. To change old and outdated ways of operating, missions must be adopted from the board of directors down to each chapter member.  
**Objectives**|  Objectives define whether a mission has been accomplished, usually quantified in the form of key performance indicators (KPIs). To maximize the chances of meeting certain objectives, chapters can adopt the SMART goal attainment strategy.  
**Priority Scale (H,M,L)** |  | **Priority** | **Meaning** | **Description**  
---|---|---  
H - High | Must have | Describes an item the chapter must do to be considered a success based on the chapter’s vision for the future  
M - Medium | Should have | Represents an item that should be included in annual business plans but could be added in later years or be satisfied in other ways if necessary   
L - Low | Nice to have | Describes an item which is considered desirable but not necessary. This will be included if time and resources permit.   
  
**Strategies**|  As objectives guide missions, so too do strategies guide objectives. If the goal of a taxi company was to increase revenue by $10 million annually, a potential strategy may include expanding the service into five new cities by the end of the year.  
**Strategic Action Programs**|  Larger programs the organization might create with defined leadership and goals to to support one or more objectives, i.e. Chapter Mentoring Program, Sponsorship Program, Certification Program, Business Analysis Development Day Program, etc. The need for the program is typically defined in the Strategic Plan, but the supporting tactics will be defined by the program team once it’s established within the Strategic Plan period.  
**Summative Evaluation**|  Summative Evaluation shows what **_has, or has not, been achieved_** insofar as the strategic plan is concerned. **Frequency/Criteria/Target** How often, and against which criteria, for whom, and with respect to which, if any, goals and policies will the final evaluation occur? **Reporting Evaluation** To what extent has your operational plan and the various annual reports prepared addressed the strategic plan?**Data Management** How can the data gathered when completing the summative evaluation be used for informing the next cycle of strategic planning?**Final Assessment and Management** How valid are the tenets the team has operated under for the duration of the strategic plan? Do they still apply, and if not, why should they be revised?**Note:** Some chapters choose to insert a copy of a scorecard they create and maintain to validate their 3-year vision is being met instead of writing out the above detail. **SWOT Analysis**|  SWOT (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats) analysis is a framework used to evaluate a company's competitive position and to develop strategic planning. SWOT analysis assesses internal and external factors, as well as current and future potential.A SWOT analysis is designed to facilitate a realistic, fact-based, data-driven look at the strengths and weaknesses of an organization, initiatives, or within its industry. The organization needs to keep the analysis accurate by avoiding preconceived beliefs or gray areas and instead focusing on real-life contexts. Chapters should use it as a guide and not necessarily as a prescription.![]()<https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/swot.asp>We chose SWOT for this template, but there are other popular methods chapters can use to understand their organizational environment if they choose to. Some include: 

  * PESTLE (political, economic, social, technological, legal and environmental) analysis
  * Scenario Planning
  * Critical success factor analysis
  * Porter's Five Forces framework

**Tactics**|  Tactics encompass the specific, low-level actions that are taken for strategies to be fulfilled.   
**Vision**|  Vision encompasses ideas that summarize where a chapter sees itself in the future. Where will it operate? Which target audience will it serve? How will it position itself against the competition? What does the chapter want to be known for? The answers to these questions should inspire and challenge the chapter to do better without being completely unattainable.  
**VMOST**|  The strategic method used to create the Strategic Plan template. The VMOST Analysis is a tool that allows a business to evaluate its core strategies in terms of whether the supporting activities of that strategy are being carried out. The VMOST analysis tries to answer that by looking at five core elements: vision, mission, objectives, strategies, and tactics. ![]()<https://brendanbroadhead.wixsite.com/vmost/what-is-vmost><https://fourweekmba.com/vmost-analysis/>

# VISION

The vision for the East Tennessee chapter is to be the trusted hub for business analysis excellence—empowering professionals to connect, grow, and lead change within their careers and communities.

# MISSION

The mission for the East Tennessee chapter is to make business analysis accessible to all by fostering a collaborative environment where members can develop practical skills, build strong professional connections, and contribute to a dynamic community of practitioners.

# EVALUATION

The following evaluation methods will show how the Program’s evaluation will be completed. 

## Formative Evaluation

The section describes the East Tennessee chapter’s evaluation method and/or process it will use to perform a formative evaluation on how the chapter is progressing toward their strategic plan objectives to date. 

The East Tennessee chapter will perform it’s formative evaluation in the following manner:

  * Use mid-year surveys and annual member feedback to assess engagement, satisfaction, and value.
  * Evaluate the status of objectives across all focus areas and adjust tactics as needed.
  * Share updates during chapter meetings and newsletters to maintain transparency and accountability.
  * Leverage findings to prioritize high-impact actions and discontinue low-performing initiatives.



### Summative Evaluation

The section describes the East Tennessee chapter’s evaluation method and/or process it will use to perform a summative evaluation on how the chapter is progressing toward their strategic plan objectives at the end of the 3 year period. 

The East Tennessee chapter will perform its formative evaluation in the following manner:

# Complete a comprehensive review of strategic goals and KPIs at the end of each quarter, year, and the strategic cycle (Q4 2027).

# Assess alignment of annual reports, financial outcomes, and operational decisions with the strategic objectives.

# Use membership analytics, event outcomes, and sponsorship performance to inform long-term decisions.

# Review the ongoing relevance of the chapter’s mission, vision, and strategic assumptions.

# Apply insights to guide the next strategic planning cycle and ensure continuity of successful programs.
