# **IIBA East Tennessee**

## **Board Role Support & Operating Expectations (2026 Pilot Year)**

## **1\. Purpose & 2026 Context**

**Purpose  
** This document defines how each board role supports the 2026 roadmap and establishes realistic time commitments and operating expectations for a pilot year focused on learning, trust-building, and sustainability.

**  
**2026 is a pilot and discovery year. Programs, partnerships, and expectations are intentionally designed to remain lightweight, adaptable, and sustainable while the chapter validates its career-focused roadmap.

## **2\. Universal Expectations for All Board Members**

### **Weekly Time Commitment**

**Standard expectation:** 1–2 hours per week on average  
Includes:

  * Checking and responding to board communications
  * Attending monthly board meetings
  * Contributing to initiatives aligned to your role
  * Attending chapter events when feasible



### **Peak Weeks**

Certain periods will require **3–4 hours** instead of 1–2, such as:

  * The week before a major event
  * Career Accelerator launch weeks
  * Sponsorship outreach periods
  * AGM preparation and annual reporting  




**  
**The chapter will avoid scheduling more than one major peak initiative within the same 2–3 week window whenever possible.  


### **Meetings**

  * Monthly board meeting (60 minutes)
  * Occasional ad-hoc working sessions (rare and scheduled in advance)
  * Attend chapter events when feasible (target: ~50%), or ensure coverage/support is coordinated when attendance is not possible



## **President**

**Primary Role in 2026:  
** **Strategic owner, external face, and scope protector**

### **Core Responsibilities**

  * Own the 2026 roadmap integrity (prevent scope creep)
  * Lead Q1 discovery:
    * Conduct/select member and employer interviews
    * Synthesize insights into decisions
  * Maintain executive relationships with:
    * Pilot
    * Clayton Homes
    * Regal
  * Ensure alignment across:
    * Strategy
    * Business plan
    * Roadmap execution



### **What the President Does _Not_ Own**

  * Day-to-day program logistics
  * Session delivery
  * Marketing execution



**Success Signal**

  * Board stays focused on MVP
  * Employers trust the chapter
  * Clear 2027 recommendations emerge



## **VP Marketing**

**Primary Role in 2026:

### **Core Responsibilities**

  * Translate roadmap into clear external messaging
  * Promote:
    * Career Accelerator sessions
    * ECBA cohort
    * CIO event
  * Ensure consistent positioning:

  * Amplify outcomes post-events:

### **What Marketing Does _Not_ Own**

  * Program design
  * Employer negotiation
  * Sponsorship sales



**Success Signal**

  * Strong attendance
  * Clear expectations set
  * No confusion about “job placement”

### **What Professional Development Does _Not_ Own**

## **Treasurer**

**Primary Role in 2026:  
** **Financial guardrails and sustainability**

### **Core Responsibilities**

  * Ensure roadmap fits **approved budget**
  * Track costs for:
    * Events
    * ECBA cohort
    * CIO event
  * Advise on:
    * Sponsorship acceptability
    * In-kind contributions
  * Ensure compliance with IIBA and nonprofit requirements
  * Provide visibility into:
    * Cost per program
    * Financial sustainability signals



### **What the Treasurer Does _Not_ Own**

  * Sponsor outreach
  * Program delivery
  * Strategic prioritization



**Success Signal**

  * No budget surprises
  * Clean financial reporting
  * Clear data for 2027 decisions



## **VP of Technology**

**Time Commitment**

  * Weekly: ~1 hour
  * Peak Weeks: 2–3 hours during:  

    * New tool setup
    * Event platform changes
    * CIO event or ECBA cohort technical needs



**Primary Responsibilities**

  * Maintain and support core chapter tools (e.g., website, email, Zoom, shared drives)
  * Ensure event technology runs smoothly (registration links, virtual access, recordings)
  * Support lightweight data capture for:  

    * Attendance
    * Surveys
    * Feedback (no analytics platforms)  

  * Advise the board on technology feasibility and risk
  * Decommission unused or redundant tools



**Does Not Own**

  * Program design
  * Data analysis or reporting
  * Marketing content  

  * Member engagement strategy



**Success in 2026 Looks Like**

  * Events run without technical issues
  * Tools are stable, documented, and easy to hand off
  * No new tech debt introduced



## **VP of Membership**

**Time Commitment**

  * Weekly: 1–2 hours
  * Peak Weeks: ~3 hours during:  

    * Career Accelerator launch
    * CIO event
    * AGM preparation



**Primary Purpose (2026)  
** Strengthen member experience, inclusion, and retention during a pilot year of career-focused programming.

**Primary Responsibilities**

  * Act as the member advocate in board discussions
  * Support Q1 discovery by:
    * Helping recruit interview participants
    * Encouraging survey participation
  * Monitor member engagement signals:
    * Attendance
    * Repeat participation
    * Informal feedback
  * Coordinate light-touch onboarding for new members
  * Support welcoming and inclusive experiences at events



**Does Not Own**

  * Program delivery
  * Certification execution
  * Sponsorship
  * Marketing campaigns



**Success in 2026 Looks Like**

  * Members understand what the chapter offers (and what it does not)
  * New members feel welcomed and oriented
  * Member feedback is surfaced early, not after disengagement
