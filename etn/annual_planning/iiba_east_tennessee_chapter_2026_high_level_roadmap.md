## **IIBA East Tennessee Chapter - 2026 High-Level Roadmap**

## **Positioning Statement**

 _2026 is a deliberate pilot year focused on learning, trust-building, and relationship development. The chapter is testing a limited set of career programs, including drop-in career programming, one certification cohort, and a joint CIO-level career event to responsibly inform future scale toward the 2028 vision._

### **Context**

  * **2026 Focus:** Pilot year for career programming and relationship development
  * **Operating Model (2026):**
    * **Career Accelerator:** Ongoing, drop-in program
    * **Certification:** ECBA cohort
    * **Signature Collaboration:** Joint CIO-level event with regional employers
    * **Networking:** Structured, career-focused (embedded within the CIO event _or_ delivered as a separate session)



## **2026 Strategic Objectives**

  1. Establish the chapter as a credible, low-friction career resource for job seekers.
  2. Pilot a drop-in career development program to test demand and formats.
  3. Run one ECBA certification cohort to validate cohort-based learning.
  4. Build trusted, repeat relationships with hiring managers and senior leaders.
  5. Establish a board-facing leadership and governance enablement foundation to support roadmap execution and future scale.
  6. Deliver a high-visibility joint CIO event with Pilot, Clayton Homes, and Regal.
  7. Capture learning to inform 2027–2028 scaling decisions.



## **Program Structure (2026)**

### **A. Career Accelerator Program (Drop-In)**

  * Branded umbrella for career readiness
  * No cohort, no required sequence
  * Participants attend sessions independently



**Session Types (examples):**

  * Career mapping & role exploration
  * Resume & LinkedIn labs
  * Mock interviews
  * Problem-solving / case workshops
  * Requirements fundamentals
  * “Skills Not Titles” practitioner panels
  * Career retrospectives from senior analysts and hiring managers
  * Hiring-manager perspective sessions



**Outputs:**

  * Skill development
  * Professional visibility
  * Relationship building
  * Participation tracking (non-credentialed)



### **B. ECBA Certification Cohort**

  * Single, time-boxed cohort in 2026
  * Fixed start/end and weekly cadence
  * Clear attendance and completion expectations



**Outputs:**

  * ECBA exam readiness
  * Peer accountability
  * Certification-aligned learning signal



### **C. Governance & Leadership & Board Support (Board-Facing)**

**Required: Governance Enablement**

  * One board-facing governance workshop
  * Recurring board meetings and 1:1s
  * Defined roles, decision rights, and communication norms



**Optional: Leadership Skill Development**

  * Optional leadership workshops or learning resources
  * Focused on facilitation, communication, and feedback
  * No required attendance
  * No credentialing or advancement implications



**Optional: Board Mentorship (Self-Directed)**

  * Board members may independently seek mentors
  * The chapter does not manage, assign, or track mentorship relationships
  * Support may include guidance and reflection prompts only



### **D. Joint CIO Career Event**

### **Partners:**

  * Pilot Company
  * Clayton Homes
  * Regal Cineworld



**Purpose:**

  * Provide executive-level insight into skills, hiring, and career growth
  * Strengthen employer relationships
  * Serve as the career capstone experience for 2026 programming



**Core Component:**

  * CIO panel with moderated Q&A focused on:
    * Skills leaders value
    * Career progression
    * Hiring signals and pitfalls
    * Industry differences



### **F. Structured Career Networking (Flexible Design)**

Networking may be delivered in one of two ways:

**Option 1 — Embedded in CIO Event**

  * Speed networking or facilitated rotations immediately following the panel
  * Small-group, role- or topic-based conversations with leaders and managers



**Option 2 — Separate Career Networking Session**

  * Standalone, structured networking event scheduled adjacent to (but not during) the CIO panel
  * Focused on hiring-manager access and peer connection



**Outputs (either option):**

  * Direct career conversations
  * Relationship building
  * No job guarantees, screening, or referrals



## **Employer & Partner Engagement (2026)**

  * Employers participate as:
    * Panelists
    * Resume reviewers
    * Mock interviewers
    * Guest speakers
    * Networking participants
  * No promises of placement, screening, or rankings
  * Any introductions are ad-hoc, opt-in, and relationship-based



## **2026 Roadmap by Quarter**

### **Q1 2026 — Discovery, Feedback & Design**

**Primary Focus: Customer Feedback and Validation, Board Governance & Support**

  * Establish recurring monthly board meetings
  * Establish recurring monthly 1:1 meetings between President and Vice Presidents
  * Confirm shared project tracker as system of record
  * Finalize roles, responsibilities, and decision rights
  * Conduct required board-facing leadership enablement workshop
  * Conduct member interviews (career stage, needs, pain points)
  * Conduct employer / hiring manager interviews
  * Launch member and partner surveys
  * Synthesize insights into:
    * Priority career needs
    * Preferred formats (drop-in vs cohort)
    * Employer expectations and constraints
  * Finalize Career Accelerator scope and branding based on findings
  * Define 6–8 validated drop-in session formats
  * Select ECBA cohort window and facilitators
  * Begin high-level planning with Pilot, Clayton Homes, and Regal for joint CIO event



**Key Outputs:**

  * Customer Feedback Summary
  * Validated 2026 pilot assumptions
  * Adjusted program designs grounded in data



### **Q2 2026 — Career Accelerator Launch**

  * Launch Career Accelerator drop-in sessions
  * Track attendance and session-level engagement
  * Invite employers to participate in individual sessions
  * Continue employer relationship development
  * Finalize CIO event format and networking approach
  * Finalize structure for CIO event and networking approach



### **Q3 2026 — ECBA Cohort + Signature Event**

  * Run ECBA certification cohort
  * Continue Career Accelerator drop-in sessions
  * Host joint CIO career event
  * Deliver structured networking (embedded or separate)
  * Observe and document employer and participant engagement



#### **Q3 2026 - Conduct governance and leadership enablement midpoint check-in**

### **Q4 2026 — Learning & Decision Point**

  * Compile 2026 Pilot Outcomes Summary:
    * Customer feedback insights
    * Drop-in session engagement
    * ECBA cohort outcomes
    * Leadership pilot feedback
    * CIO event and networking outcomes
  * Identify:
    * What to repeat in 2027
    * What should remain drop-in vs. cohort
    * Opportunities for deeper employer collaboration
  * Feed learnings into 2027 planning and 2028 future-state trajectory



## **2026 Success Indicators**

  * Board roles, decision rights, and communication norms are operating consistently with minimal escalation friction.
  * Consistent attendance across multiple drop-in sessions
  * Successful completion of one ECBA cohort
  * Execution of a joint CIO career event
  * Effective delivery of structured career networking
  * Repeat participation from employers and senior leaders
  * Clear, documented recommendations for 2027 evolution

Sheri's Feedback & Proposals

# Overall Thoughts

The roadmap is clear, structured and aligned with our long-term vision. It provides a balanced portfolio of career readiness, certification, leadership, mentorship and employer engagement. The CIO event will provide the visibility we need to elevate our chapter credibility, and the drop-in model is low friction and ideal for early-career professionals. 

An emphasis on learning and iteration are what our board needs at this stage.

Our opportunities are that the roadmap assumes a level of board capacity that may not match our reality. Meeting cadences and communication norms need to be explicitly built into the plan - including recurring meetings for all board leadership and 121 meetings with the president and vice presidents. 

All chapter leaders must go through leadership training if we want to create tiers with director-level positions in the future.

I recommend we start with the Good version of the plan (See below for Good/Better/Best options) and scale as capacity allows. Chapter leaders must complete leadership training, and we need to establish a foundation of governance in Q1 that outlines these items:

  * Recurring board meetings
  * Recurring 121 meetings with president and vice presidents
  * Shared project tracker
  * Clear roles and responsibilities
  * Communication norms
