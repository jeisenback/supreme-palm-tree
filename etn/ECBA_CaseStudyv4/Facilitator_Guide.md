# Facilitator Guide — v4: Bi-Weekly, Combined A+B

--- DOCUMENT GUIDE ---

Who it's for:   Facilitator only.
When to use it: Review before the series starts. Re-read the session section and SME notes the night before each meeting.
Restricted:     Yes — do not share with participants.

## What Is Different About v4

In v1 and v2, Session B runs 2 weeks after Session A — participants arrive with misconceptions fully formed. In v4, Session B follows Session A in the same meeting. The 2-week gap is gone.

The **commit prompt** compensates for this. Before the B segment begins, participants write one sentence committing to a position. They cannot take it back. The practitioner then engages with what they wrote. This creates the same productive tension — participants defending a position they have committed to — that the 2-week gap would have generated.

Your two most important skills in v4 are:
1. **Running the transition** from A to B cleanly — the energy shift matters
2. **Briefing the practitioner in the room** — they need to see the commit prompt responses before they open

## Before the Series Starts

### Practitioner Selection and Briefing
Confirm one practitioner before the series begins. Do not start without this.

**Ideal practitioner profile:** Someone with real BA field experience across multiple domains — not necessarily ECBA-certified, but someone who has written requirements, conducted stakeholder interviews, and been in a room where a solution recommendation was challenged. A BA, PM, senior analyst, or consultant all work.

**Initial briefing (can be done in 30 minutes):**
1. Share the full 10-week calendar — 5 dates, 2 hours each
2. Explain the format: they join for the second hour of each session (or the full 2 hours if available); their role is friction provider, not lecturer
3. Walk through the 5 B-segment exercises from the master plan — one session each (see `etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/ECBA_CaseStudy_Plan.md`)
4. Explain the commit prompt: *"Each session, participants write one sentence during the break. You'll see those responses on a screen or sheet before you open. Use them as your entry point — react to what they actually wrote, not a hypothetical."*
5. Share the full SME prep guide (`etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/Facilitator/Session_B_SME_Prep_Guide.md`)

**15-minute check-in before each session** (can be a message or brief call):
- Send a 2-sentence context note: *"The group is [strong/struggling] with [X]. Watch for [Y]. The stretch question tonight is [Z]."*
- Confirm the practitioner has the commit prompt question for the session

### Session 0 Distribution
- Distribute `ECBA_CaseStudyv4/Session0_Onboarding_Handout.md` one week before Session 1
- In your distribution message: *"Each session is 2 hours. The first 75 minutes cover concepts and case work. The second 45 minutes are practitioner-led — a returning BA practitioner will join every session to challenge your thinking. Come ready to work the full two hours."*

## The Commit Prompt — How It Works

The commit prompt is the structural mechanism that replaces the 2-week gap. It must be run correctly to work.

### Timing
The commit prompt is distributed during the 5-minute break between Session A and Session B. Do not skip the break — it is not just for logistics. Participants need 60–90 seconds of transition before the practitioner enters.

### Logistics
**Option A (in-person or hybrid):** Print one half-sheet per participant before the session. Have them face-down on a table or in a folder. Distribute at the break, ask participants to write immediately and not discuss.

**Option B (virtual):** Display the commit prompt as a full-screen slide at the break. Ask participants to type their response into a private message to you (not the group chat) or into a shared anonymous poll. Collect before the B segment opens.

### How to use the responses
At the break, collect or receive the responses. Scan them quickly. You are looking for:
- The most common answer (name this to the group)
- The two or three most divergent answers (use these as the practitioner's opening material)
- Any response that contains a fundamental misunderstanding (flag privately to the practitioner)

Before handing to the practitioner, say: *"I shared your responses with [practitioner name]. They're going to start there."*

### What to say when distributing the commit prompt
Say exactly this: *"Before we start the second half — one sentence. Write down your answer to this question. No discussion yet. You have 90 seconds."*

Then display the question. Do not explain it. Do not give examples. Wait in silence.

After 90 seconds: *"Good. I'm collecting these now. We'll start back up in [2–3 minutes]."*

## Full 2-Hour Session Run-of-Show

### 0:00–0:10 — Open (Session A)

**Session 1:** Cold ARCS open — use the base plan Session 1 script (`etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/Facilitator/Session1_Facilitator_Script.md`).

**Sessions 2–5:** Homework share-out. See Opening section in v2 Facilitator Guide for the script. Same approach applies here.

**One v4 addition:** At the open of Sessions 2–5, close the share-out by previewing the B segment: *"Tonight we're going to [describe B exercise in one sentence]. [Practitioner name] will join us for the second half. Before then, let's get into [session topic]."*

### 0:10–0:35 — Concept Instruction (Session A)
Same as v2 Facilitator Guide — checkpoint handling, TrailBlaze anchoring, pacing.

### 0:35–0:43 — Guided Application (Session A)
Same as v2 Facilitator Guide.

### 0:43–1:05 — Group Exercise (Session A)
Same as v2 Facilitator Guide — circulation, timing, stuck-group prompts.

**Energy management note:** By the 60-minute mark, participants have been working for an hour. Energy often dips here. Watch for this. If the room is low, add a 30-second physical prompt: *"Stand up, stretch, switch seats with someone from another group — you have 30 seconds."* This resets attention without losing time.

### 1:05–1:13 — Share-Out (Session A)
Compress the share-out slightly in v4 — 8 minutes total instead of 10. Each group gets 2 minutes to present. Give one specific piece of feedback per group. Say: *"We're going to pick up on this again in a few minutes with [practitioner name] — your work here is the starting point."*

**Do not close the share-out with final conclusions.** Leave one unresolved question hanging: *"Here's what I want you to hold onto — we haven't answered [X] yet. [Practitioner] is going to have thoughts about that."* This creates anticipation for the B segment.

### 1:13–1:21 — Practice Round (Session A)
4 questions, 2 minutes each. Follow the v2 Facilitator Guide timing for this phase. Do not skip — exam prep is the purpose of the series.

### 1:21–1:28 — Transition to Session B

This transition is the most important moment in v4. Get it right.

**Step 1 (0:30):** Say: *"Take a 5-minute break — back at [time]."*

**Step 2 (during break):** Distribute the commit prompt. Collect responses. Scan them. Brief the practitioner in 60–90 seconds: *"Here's what the room said. The most common answer was [X]. Two people said [Y]. Watch for [Z]."* Hand them the responses or show them on your device.

**Step 3 (at restart, 0:30):** Say: *"Welcome back. This is [practitioner name]. They've read your responses. [Practitioner] — it's yours."*

Then step back. Physically move away from the front of the room. You are now a timekeeper, not a co-facilitator.

### 1:28–1:31 — Commit Prompt + Practitioner Opening (Session B)

The practitioner opens with the commit prompt responses. They should name what they saw across the responses, pick 1–2 contrasting answers, and use those as their starting point.

**What to do if the practitioner does not use the responses:** After 3–4 minutes, say quietly: *"There were a couple of interesting responses in the batch — [quote one anonymously] — does that change anything for you?"* This redirects without interrupting.

### 1:31–1:55 — Practitioner Exercise (Session B)

Your role: watch the clock. Signal at the 20-minute mark (hold up a hand or a card). Signal again at 25 minutes. At 28 minutes, step in: *"[Practitioner name] — we have about 5 minutes left in the exercise. Can we start heading toward the debrief?"*

**Do not let the exercise run into the debrief time.** The debrief is what converts the experience into transferable learning. A truncated debrief is the most common mistake in combined-format sessions.

### 1:55–2:03 — Debrief (Session B)

Say: *"[Practitioner name] — step out of character for a second. What were you actually doing in there?"*

Then let them answer. Do not speak while they are speaking. After they finish, ask the group: *"What did you hear that you didn't expect?"* Take 2 responses.

**If the debrief stalls (practitioner is modest, gives a short answer):** Ask: *"What was the hardest answer you got from the group tonight — the one that surprised you?"* Or: *"When you've been in this situation for real — as the BA or the stakeholder — what usually goes wrong?"*

### 2:03–2:08 — Stretch Question + Close (Session B)

The practitioner asks the stretch question (they have it from the prep guide and your pre-session check-in). After the question is asked: *"Take 30 seconds. Write down your best current answer. You'll use this in the next session's share-out."*

After 30 seconds: ask 1 person to share their current thinking. This is a preview, not a final answer — say so.

Thank the practitioner. Assign homework (read the prompt aloud, including the stretch question as the challenge task). Send the 2-minute survey. Close.

## Managing the 2-Hour Energy Arc

Most participants have never sat in a focused 2-hour learning session. Energy follows a predictable curve:
- Minutes 0–30: engaged
- Minutes 30–60: beginning to tire
- Minutes 60–75: energy dip
- Minutes 75–90: second wind (new person in room + new format resets attention)
- Minutes 90–120: generally sustained if the B segment has momentum

**What to watch for:**
- Slouching, side conversations, or phone-checking after the 50-minute mark → add a physical break, or split the group exercise differently
- Short group exercise outputs → the group is low on energy, not low on knowledge; add a re-energizing prompt before share-out
- Practitioner going abstract → step in and ground them: *"Can you make that specific to TrailBlaze?"*

## If the Practitioner Cannot Attend

Confirm by 48 hours before. If they cancel within 48 hours, run the B segment yourself.

**Script for facilitator-as-practitioner:**

*"Our practitioner had a conflict tonight. I'm going to run the second half differently — I'm going to put on a different hat for the next 30 minutes. I'm not going to be your facilitator. I'm going to be [the stakeholder/CEO/SME character for this session's B exercise]. Same exercise, same questions. Let's go."*

Then run the exercise from the B segment format in the master plan. Step out of character for the debrief: *"Okay — I'm your facilitator again. Here's what I was trying to surface in that exercise..."* Ask the stretch question as yourself, not the character.

This is not as effective as a practitioner, but it is better than canceling the B segment.

## Common Problems and Responses

### Participants write nothing for the commit prompt.
Do not skip to the B segment. Say: *"I'm going to give you 60 more seconds. One sentence — your honest current answer, not the right answer. Go."* If someone still does not write, that is fine — the majority of responses are enough. Do not call out non-responses.

### The B segment runs 15 minutes long.
Cut the debrief from 8 minutes to 4 minutes. Ask only: *"What were you doing and why?"* Then move immediately to the stretch question. Send the full debrief questions by email after the session.

### Participants are exhausted at the 90-minute mark.
Before the B segment opens, say: *"This is the second half. The format changes completely — [practitioner name] is here and they're going to work with what you just produced. This is the part that's different from any textbook prep you've done."* Novelty resets attention. The practitioner's presence and the commit prompt response moment reliably re-engage the room.

### The practitioner lectures instead of applying friction.
After 5 minutes of lecture, step in: *"[Name] — I want to make sure we get into the group's work. Can you put the group in a situation where they have to defend something?"* This redirects without being confrontational.

### Participants want to discuss the stretch question instead of just writing it.
Shut it down for 30 seconds: *"Write first. Discussion in a minute."* Then take 1–2 responses. If discussion opens up organically after that, let it run briefly — but close it: *"Hold this for next session. I want to hear your refined answer when you've had time to think."*

## Timing Recovery Cheat Sheet

In a 2-hour session, timing errors compound. If you are running behind, cut in this order:

| Phase to cut | Time saved | What you lose |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| Homework share-out: 10 min → 7 min | 3 min | Fewer responses |
| Guided application: 8 min → 5 min | 3 min | Less modeling |
| Share-out: 8 min → 5 min | 3 min | Shorter feedback |
| Practice round: 8 min → 4 min (2 Qs) | 4 min | Assign 2 Qs as homework |
| B debrief: 8 min → 4 min | 4 min | Less reflection time |

Do not cut the commit prompt. Do not cut the practitioner exercise below 20 minutes. Do not skip the stretch question — it is the bridge to the next session.
