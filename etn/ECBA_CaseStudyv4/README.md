# ECBA Case Study — Format v4: Bi-Weekly, Combined A+B

--- DOCUMENT GUIDE ---
What this is:   Format variant of the TrailBlaze Adventures ECBA Case Study series. Describes how to run the 5-session program bi-weekly with Session A and B content merged into a single 2-hour session per cycle — no separate SME scheduling required.
Who it's for:   Facilitator; program coordinator; board approver.
When to use it: When the chapter wants the full A+B content experience without the logistics of recruiting 5 separate SME speakers and holding 10 separate meetings. Best suited for cohorts where a single returning expert (or the facilitator themselves with practitioner experience) can fill the SME role across all five sessions.
Relationship:   Content is identical to the base series (etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/). Session B exercises are compressed and embedded at the end of each 2-hour session. Schedule and logistics differ significantly.
Restricted:     No — shareable with board and interested participants.
--- END GUIDE ---

## What Changes in This Format

| Factor | Base Series (v1 — Monthly + B) | This Format (v4 — Bi-Weekly, Combined) |
|--------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Cadence | A sessions monthly; B sessions mid-month (2 separate meetings) | One 2-hour session every 2 weeks |
| Overall duration | ~5 months | ~10 weeks |
| Total meetings | 10 (5A + 5B) | 5 |
| Session length | 90 min (A) + 60 min (B) | 120 min combined |
| SME model | 5 separate speakers, each recruited 6–8 weeks out | 1 consistent practitioner OR facilitator-as-SME |
| Homework window | ~3 weeks per cycle | ~2 weeks per cycle |
| B-session gap after A | ~2 weeks (time to process + form questions) | None — B follows A immediately |
| Who it fits | Working professionals with flexible calendars and a strong SME network | Cohorts with a strong in-house practitioner or a single returning guest; programs with limited scheduling bandwidth |

## What Does Not Change

- TrailBlaze Adventures case study, session-gated reveals, and all in-session exercises are identical.
- All five A-session artifacts (BACCM chart, stakeholder map, requirements, RTM, solution recommendation) are the same.
- ECBA domain coverage and BABOK chapter mapping are unchanged.
- The SME-led exercise from Session B is preserved inside each combined session — it is compressed, not cut.

## The Core Design Trade-Off

The base series places Session B two weeks after Session A. That gap is structural: participants arrive at Session B with misconceptions already formed, and the SME surfaces them at exactly the right moment. In v4, that gap is eliminated — the SME exercise follows directly after Session A content.

**What is lost:** The "productive struggle" period between A and B. Participants have not yet sat with their homework or formed the wrong assumptions Session B was designed to challenge.

**What is gained:** One meeting per cycle, one SME to manage, consistent practitioner voice across all five sessions, and a tighter schedule that keeps cohorts intact.

**How the design compensates:** In v4, the combined session opens the B segment with a brief prompt that forces participants to commit to a position before the SME interaction begins — replicating the moment of vulnerability that the 2-week gap would have created. Participants are told: *"Write down in one sentence what you think the answer is. You will defend it in 2 minutes."* The SME then engages with what they wrote, not what they wish they had written.

## SME Model for v4

v4 works best with **one consistent practitioner** returning across all five sessions — ideally someone who has BA field experience across multiple domains. This person builds a relationship with the cohort over 10 weeks, which increases the candor and quality of the career reflection moments that close each B segment.

If a single SME is not available, a knowledgeable facilitator with BA practitioner experience can run the B segments directly. The Session B exercises are structured enough to run without an external speaker — the SME role is a friction provider, not a lecturer.

If using a guest speaker: the same speaker briefing guide applies (`etn/ECBA_CaseStudy/Facilitator/Session_B_SME_Prep_Guide.md`). Confirm the speaker at least 3 weeks before the series starts and share the full 5-session calendar at that time.
