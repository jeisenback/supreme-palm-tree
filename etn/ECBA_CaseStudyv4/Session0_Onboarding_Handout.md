# ECBA Case Study — Session 0 Onboarding Handout (v4: Bi-Weekly, Combined A+B)

*Distribute before Session 1. No meeting required.*

---

## What This Series Is

This is a 5-session, 10-week ECBA exam preparation series built around one evolving case study: **TrailBlaze Adventures**, a mid-sized adventure travel company working through a BA engagement from first contact through pilot results.

One 2-hour session every two weeks. Each session covers both conceptual instruction (first hour) and a practitioner-led challenge segment (second hour). A returning practitioner joins every session to add real-world friction to your work.

---

## How Each Session Works

The first ~75 minutes introduce ECBA concepts and apply them to the TrailBlaze case. You will produce an artifact in every session — a real BA deliverable that builds session to session.

At the midpoint break, you will receive a single prompt. Write your answer in one sentence. This is not graded and you will not be called out by name — but your answer will shape the practitioner segment that follows. The practitioner will read several responses and engage directly with what the room produced.

The second ~45 minutes puts your thinking under pressure. A returning practitioner — someone with real BA field experience — will challenge your position, push back on your assumptions, and then step back and tell you exactly what they were doing and why. They will also answer a stretch question at the close of every session as a personal reflection.

### Homework
After each session: one short written artifact, one challenge question (drawn from the practitioner stretch question), and 6 ECBA exam-style MCQs. Total time: approximately 60–90 minutes.

The stretch question from the end of each session becomes your homework challenge. Think about it in relation to your own experience, not just the textbook answer. You may be asked to share your response at the next session's opening.

---

## How the Case Study Works

TrailBlaze Adventures is revealed in stages. You will not see the full picture until Session 5. Your decisions in Session 1 follow you into Session 3 — a problem statement without measurable criteria leaves you with nothing to trace when requirements are due. Build with that in mind.

---

## Active Learning Environment

Every session includes group exercises, share-outs, a timed multiple choice practice round, and a practitioner challenge. The practitioner challenge is not a lecture — it is an interaction. Your work in the first hour is the raw material. Come prepared to defend it.
