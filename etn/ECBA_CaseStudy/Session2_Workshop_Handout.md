## Exercise 3 — BA Approach Recommendation (20 minutes)

You have enough information to sketch a BA approach. This is not a full plan — it is the direction you recommend before the formal planning work begins.

**Your BA approach document should include:**

**1. Recommended approach type** (agile, plan-driven, or hybrid) and the reason you chose it.
- This team: 1 FE, 1 BE, 0.5 QA, 0.5 BA/PM. 10-week build window. Budget not yet formally approved.
- Consider: how much uncertainty remains? How stable are the requirements? How tolerant is the team of change mid-build?

**2. Stakeholder engagement priorities** — who do you engage first, and why?
- You cannot engage all five stakeholders equally. You have limited time. Rank the first three people you would prioritize and explain the reason for your ordering.

**3. First elicitation activity** — what is the first structured elicitation activity you would run, and why?
- Do not say "more interviews." Interviews are done for now. What specific structured activity would give you the highest-value information next?

**4. One risk** — what is one thing that could derail this engagement before the MVP is built?

**Format:** Bullet points are fine. This should be readable in under 2 minutes. It is not a formal document — it is a working recommendation.

## Rubric (12 pts)

- **Root cause analysis (0–3):** Three causes identified with supporting evidence; one cause named as most-evidenced; at least one described as "asserted without data"
- **Elicitation technique matching (0–3):** Technique matched to context, not just named; rationale is specific to the information gap
- **BA approach recommendation (0–3):** Approach type chosen with a reason; stakeholder priority justified; first elicitation activity is specific (not just "more interviews")
- **Presentation clarity (0–3):** Structured, BA-framed language; root cause and approach are clearly distinguished
