**Where we are in the TrailBlaze story:**
The MVP launched. The pilot ran for 30 days. The results are in. Some targets were hit. Some were not. This session is your chance to close the loop — trace what happened back to the decisions you made as a BA across Sessions 1–4, and figure out what you would do differently.

**The 30-day pilot results:**

| KPI | Target | Actual | Status |
|-----|--------|--------|--------|
| Total bookings | 100 | 87 | MISSED (13% below target) |
| NPS | ≥ 8.0 | 8.2 | MET |
| On-time departure rate | ≥ 95% | 91% | MISSED |

**Stakeholder reactions (collected at the 30-day review):**

- **Patricia Chu (Finance Director):** "We're on the right trajectory. I want quarterly ROI reviews going forward. The 87 vs. 100 gap is worth investigating before we call it a success." (4/5 satisfaction)
- **Sam Torres (Lead Guide Rep):** "The roster was better. The information was there. Two trips still had changes that didn't get communicated — ops dashboard updated but guides didn't check it in time. That's a behavior change problem as much as a system problem." (Not formally surveyed — informal feedback to Rachel)

**Post-pilot analysis (root cause of missed targets):**

*Bookings short of target (87 vs. 100):*
- SEO work was in scope and requirements were written, but the SEO task was not assigned to a sprint until week 8 — too late for Google indexing to take effect by launch. The feature existed but wasn't discoverable. This is a traceability gap — the SEO requirement was written but not traced to a sprint task.
- The Instagram campaign ran for 3 weeks, not 4 — Jordan's content calendar was delayed because she received the listing assets 5 days late. This was a handoff failure.

*On-time departure rate (91% vs. 95%):*
- The ops dashboard showed guide assignments and confirmations, but did not surface **same-day changes** (customer count adjustments, late cancellations). If a customer cancelled the morning of the trip, Rachel updated the dashboard — but guides were already en route and had no mechanism to receive that notification. This was an elicitation gap: the same-day change scenario was discussed in the workshops but was not captured as a requirement.

*Guide assignment confirmation (78% < 24 hrs):*
- The notification went out automatically. But "notification sent" was the acceptance criterion, not "notification acknowledged." 22% of trips had guides who did not acknowledge via the dashboard before the 24-hour window — some due to connectivity issues in the field, some due to not having adopted the new workflow. This is an acceptance criteria problem.

## Practice Round — Integrated Exam Simulation (20 minutes)

8–10 multiple choice questions across all 9 ECBA domains. Individual answers first (no discussion), then group debrief.

*Questions distributed by the facilitator — see Session5_PracticeQuestions.md.*

**What's different about Session 5 questions:**
- Scenarios are longer and more integrated — multiple ECBA domains in one question
- Wrong answers are more plausible — the exam is testing whether you can distinguish between two things that both sound right
- Some questions require you to know what the BA should NOT do, not just what they should do

**Exam tip:** If you read a question and two answers both seem defensible, look at what each answer is asking the BA to prioritize. The ECBA generally prioritizes: understanding the need before choosing a solution; engaging stakeholders before making decisions; verifying before recommending. If one answer does those things and the other skips them, the answer that does them is almost always correct.

## You Made It Through the Series

You have now applied BA thinking across the full lifecycle of one engagement: from symptom to problem statement, stakeholder identification through requirements conversion, solution analysis through traceability, and pilot results through retrospective.

The exam tests whether you can do these things in 2-minute scenario questions under time pressure. You have done them in 90-minute sessions under collaborative pressure, which is harder. Use that.

**Before your ECBA exam:**
1. Review your complete artifact set from all 5 sessions
2. Re-do all 30 homework MCQs without looking at the answers — check your accuracy, then study the rationales for every question you got wrong
3. Practice translating what you did in these sessions into ECBA domain language — every exercise maps to a domain; every artifact maps to a BABOK knowledge area
4. Focus extra time on any domain where you felt less confident in the practice rounds
