Slide 6 — New TrailBlaze Prompt (read aloud, do not distribute)

> The CEO called an all-hands yesterday. She opened with: "I've already talked to the board. We're building a mobile app. We need to show progress by Q3."
>
> The Head of Operations said nothing in the meeting. Afterwards they told two team members: "The app isn't the problem. We've had three guides quit in the last two months and nobody upstairs knows it."

> Facilitator note: Do not distribute this as a handout. Read it aloud or show it on screen. The group works from memory and notes — just like a real brief.

Slide 7 — Live BACCM Workshop (30 min)
Groups of 3–4. Same groups as Session 1A if possible.

**The task:**
Using only the BACCM — apply each element to the new prompt. The SME is in the room and will push back.

| BACCM Element | Question to work | Common trap |
|---|---|---|
| **Change** | What transformation is being requested — and by whom? | Accepting the CEO's framing without challenge |
| **Need** | What is actually broken? What evidence do you have? | Conflating a symptom with a root cause |
| **Solution** | Can you fill this in yet? Why or why not? | Filling it in prematurely because the CEO already announced one |
| **Stakeholder** | Who has standing in this change — including those not in the room? | Missing the Head of Operations, Lead Guide, the guides who quit |
| **Value** | What does success look like, and who defines it? | Measuring only what the CEO can see |
| **Context** | What is forcing this conversation right now? | Ignoring board pressure and guide attrition as separate context inputs |

**SME participates as themselves:** Comments on approach, asks probing questions, challenges where real-world experience differs from what the group assumes.

**Key facilitator interrupt (use if group fills in SOLUTION too quickly):**
> "The CEO has already announced the solution. Does that mean the BA's SOLUTION box is filled? What is the BA's job here?"

Slide 8 — Share-Out + SME Response (built into the 30 min block)
- Each group gives 60 seconds on their NEED and VALUE entries — those are the two most contested
- SME responds to each: "On a real engagement, here's how that plays out..."
- Facilitator listens for: groups who defaulted to the CEO's framing, groups who surfaced the guide attrition signal

Facilitator redirect if groups converge too quickly:
> "Everyone agreed on the same NEED. Is that reassuring or is it a warning sign?"

Slide 9 — Debrief: What the Model Does Under Pressure (10 min)
Questions for the group — SME answers alongside:

1. **The solution was announced before the BA was engaged. What is the BA's move?**
   - Not to stop the app — to ensure the NEED is defined before the app is scoped
   - BACCM gives you a professional frame for that conversation

2. **The Head of Operations said nothing in the meeting. Is that person a stakeholder? What kind?**
   - High influence, currently disengaged — the most dangerous quadrant
   - Guide attrition is a context signal that changes the BACCM, not a separate problem

3. **How do you document competing stakeholder diagnoses without picking a side?**
   - NEED stays open until elicitation confirms it
   - The BACCM does not demand consensus — it demands traceability

> **Open Q&A:** 5 minutes — direct questions to the SME. Real work only.

---

Slide 10 — What the BACCM Is Actually For
- The BACCM is your route map — not the summit flag
- When a solution is announced before a need is defined, the BACCM is how a BA asks: "How do we know that route leads to the right summit?"
- When stakeholders disagree, the BACCM shows you where the disagreement lives — usually in NEED or VALUE, not in SOLUTION
- When the terrain is noisy, the BACCM separates what is driving the change from what is being changed
- Exam framing: ECBA questions about BACCM often test whether you can identify the correct element — especially when the scenario is designed to push you toward SOLUTION before NEED is confirmed
