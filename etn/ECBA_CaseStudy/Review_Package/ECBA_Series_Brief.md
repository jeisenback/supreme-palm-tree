## What This Is

A five-session Entry Certificate in Business Analysis (ECBA) certification prep series for International Institute of Business Analysis (IIBA) East Tennessee Chapter members. One case study — TrailBlaze Adventures — runs the full length of the series. Each session maps to two or three of the nine ECBA exam domains and anchors every concept to an advancing business analysis (BA) scenario. The series targets working professionals preparing for the ECBA exam on a monthly schedule.

| 2 | May | Implementing BA, Context | 90-min Session A + 60-min Session B |
| 3 | June | Change, Need | 90-min Session A + 60-min Session B |
| 4 | July | Solution, Stakeholder | 90-min Session A + 60-min Session B |
| 5 | August | Value + integrated review of all 9 domains | 90-min Session A + 60-min Session B |

**Session A (90 min):** Facilitator-led. Concepts, guided application, group exercise, timed practice multiple choice questions (MCQs), homework assignment.

**Session B (60 min, mid-cycle):** Subject matter expert (SME) co-facilitated. Same case study, no new content. The SME adds real-world friction to what the group already built. Format varies by session: mock stakeholder interview, live workshop, solution challenge, or retrospective.

The series covers all 9 ECBA exam domains. Each domain maps explicitly to a Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK) V3 Knowledge Area — both lenses are labeled in the master plan.

## Learning Design Rationale

- **Case study continuity:** TrailBlaze context advances session by session. Participants only get what the current session has unlocked. Each session picks up where the last one ended — participants make decisions on incomplete information, which is how real BA work operates.
- **Motivational structure:** Each session opens with an Attention → Relevance → Confidence → Satisfaction (ARCS) sequence. It bridges what the group just presented to the new concept before instruction starts.
- **3-tier practice model:** 2 checkpoint MCQs mid-concept, 4 timed MCQs at session close, 6 MCQs as homework. All follow ECBA exam style — scenario-based, application not recall.
- **SME integration:** Each Session B brings in a practitioner who has been in the scenario. They push back on the group's work in real time, then step out of character and debrief on how that situation actually plays out. Participants leave with a contact, not just a concept.
- **Brand alignment:** All participant-facing materials follow the IIBA East Tennessee Chapter style guide — colors, typography, voice principles, and banned word list.
