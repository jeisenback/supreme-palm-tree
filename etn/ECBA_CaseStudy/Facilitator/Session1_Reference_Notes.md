## Transcript Summary — David Thomson (BACCM & BAPM)

Key points from David Thomson's BACCM and BA Planning & Monitoring session:
- Use the BACCM as the organizing frame for all BA work — every concept maps back to one of the six elements
- Define governance early; unclear governance is how a 3-month project becomes a 3-year project
- Use lightweight artifacts: stakeholder map, RACI, RTM — don't over-engineer before you have a confirmed need
- Expect UAT to surface new requirements; build a process for handling them before they arrive
- Measure BA effectiveness by: accuracy of requirements, completeness of coverage, stakeholder satisfaction

## Application Notes for Session 1

- The BACCM guided exercise (Slide 8) should take no more than 7 minutes — if groups are stuck on Solution, that's the teaching moment. Don't rush past it.
- Checkpoint 1 (after Slide 7) is the most diagnostic checkpoint in the session — if participants can't map TrailBlaze to BACCM elements, the group exercise will stall. Spend an extra 2 minutes if needed.
- Checkpoint 2 (after Slide 10) tests whether participants distinguish stakeholder types from stakeholder roles — a common ECBA confusion.
- The "hold Solution blank" discipline in Slide 8 is intentional: it sets up all of Session 2's planning work. Reinforce it explicitly.
