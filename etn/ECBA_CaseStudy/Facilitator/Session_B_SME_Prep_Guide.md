## What You're Walking Into

This is a 60-minute case study deep dive session. The group has already covered the core concepts in Session A, two weeks ago. Your job is not to teach — it's to make the material feel real.

You will co-facilitate a hands-on exercise using **TrailBlaze Adventures**, the fictional company this study group has been working with all series. The group knows the case well. You'll add texture, real-world pushback, and honest reflection from your own experience.

**You do not need to know ECBA certification content.** The facilitator handles exam framing. You just need to show up as a practitioner.

You will:
1. Share **2–3 minutes** of relevant background from your own work — one specific moment where the session topic made or broke a project

## What Makes a Great Session B

- **Disagree with the textbook if your experience differs** — that is what the group needs to hear
- **Use specific examples**: "On the inventory project at [Company], we found that..." is more valuable than general advice
- **When the group struggles, use questions rather than answers**: "What would happen if you did it that way on a real project?"
- **Be honest about mistakes** — the group learns more from failure stories than success stories
- **You don't need to wrap things up neatly** — leaving productive tension is fine; the facilitator will close

**The one thing to avoid:** Giving the group a solution before they've had a chance to work it out. Your job is to add friction and reality, not to rescue them.

---

## Phrases That Help

If you want to redirect the group toward realism:
> "On a real project, that would last about 5 minutes before [X] happened."

If you want to validate their instinct but add nuance:
> "That's the right move — here's what usually goes wrong with it."

If you want to play a character and push back:
> "As [Jordan/Marcus/Priya], I'd say: prove it. Show me the data."
