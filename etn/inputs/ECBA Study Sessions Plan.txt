Preparing to Study
Preparing to Study
* Sleep - Maintain a sleep schedule
* Exercise - 2.5 hours of cardio, 2 days of strength training each week
* Hydration - Keep water with you while studying
* Nutrition - Fruits, seeds, oatmeal, green tea, dark chocolate
* Put your phone away 1 hour before bed
* Use the pomodoro method for study sessions - 25 minutes focus time, 5 minutes break, 4 repetitions
   * After 4 repetitions, extend breaks to 15 minutes


How to Take Notes
* Cornell notes
   * Notes section: Capture the gist of what you’ve learned and the important details in your own words
   * Cue section: 
      * Used simultaneously or after you take your notes
      * Add questions that will help your organize or remember the material
      * Can help you identify what you still need to study more
   * Summary section: 
      * Share what you learned in no more than three sentences
      * Write this while the information’s still fresh in your mind
      * Start your next study session by reviewing this area
  

How to Study
* It’s better to spend 10 minutes every evening reviewing information than it is to spend a long time in a single study session.
* Test yourself from memory each study session
   * Retrieval practice - Write it out
      * Write or sketch everything you can remember from memory
      * Put the information in your own words
      * Come up with your own examples
      * Make connections to your own life
      * Finish by reviewing the course materials and seeing what you got right
      * Use flashcards
      * Create concept maps
      * Provide concrete examples
* Dual coding - Combine visuals with words
   * Sketch your notes
* Interleave topics - Alternate study of three topics in the same subject area to create space for forgetting and remembering
* Elaboration - Works best with prior knowledge
   * Ask “how” or “why” questions
   * Explain concepts to yourself
   * Explain how concepts are similar and how they’re different
Teaching
* Follow the ARCS motivational model for content delivery
   * Attention: Capture learners’ interest and engage them
      * Start with a surprising scenario: Present a failed project and ask learners to guess what went wrong. Reveal that the root cause was poor requirements or unclear stakeholder needs.
      * Use an interactive poll: Which of these is not a business analyst’s responsibility?
      * Incorporate multimedia: Short videos showing analysts in action, dashboard that illustrates how insights drive decisions.
      * Tell a story: A narrative about a company that transformed through good analysis.
   * Relevance: Make the learning material meaningful and applicable to learners’ lives
      * Tie concepts to real-world roles: Show how business analysis skills apply to project management, product ownership, operations, etc.
      * Use industry examples: Demonstrate how BA techniques (Like SWOT, process mapping, user stories) show up in everyday business decisions.
      * Let learners personalize tasks: Invite them to choose a company or domain they care about and apply BA tools to it.
      * Connect to future opportunities: Highlight career paths, certifications and the growing demand for analytical thinking.
   * Confidence: Build learners’ belief in their ability to succeed
      * Start with small wins: Begin with simple tasks like identifying stakeholders or drafting a basic problem statement.
      * Provide guided practice: Use templates, step-by-step walkthroughs and worked examples before asking them to do it independently.
      * Offer clear success criteria: Rubrics for user stories, process maps or requirement statements help learners know what “good” looks like.
      * Encourage peer collaboration: Group activities reduce pressure and let learners learn from each other.
   * Satisfaction: Ensure learners feel a sense of accomplishment and reward from their learning experience
      * Authentic application: End the lesson with a mini-project where learners analyze a real or simulated business problem using the skills they just learned.
* ARCS in action:
   * Attention - Open with a case study of a multimillion-dollar project failure caused by unclear requirements.
   * Relevance - Connect the case to personal workplace experiences.
   * Confidence - Teach a simple technique like writing a problem statement and let learners practice with a template.
   * Satisfaction - Have learners share their statements, give feedback and reflect on how this skill could prevent the kind of failure shown at the start of the lesson.
 
Five-Month Delivery Format
Sessions Timeline
* Session 1 - April
* Session 2 - May
* Session 3 - June
* Session 4 - July
* Session 5 - August
The ECBA’s Nine Domains
1. Understanding Business Analysis
2. Mindset for Effective Business Analysis
3. Implementing Business Analysis
4. Change
5. Need
6. Solution
7. Stakeholder
8. Value
9. Context
Session 1 - Foundations & Core Concepts
Topics
* Understanding Business Analysis
   * What BA is
   * Role in enabling change
   * The BACCM
   * Assessing value
   * Any other additional items
* Mindset for Effective Business Analysis
   * BA mindset
   * Values
   * Principles
   * Foundational competencies
Case Study
* Analyze a fictional organization and identify:
   * Business problems
   * Stakeholders involved
   * Where BA provides value
* Apply the BACCM to frame the situation
Session 2 - Applying BA in Practice
Topics
* Implementing Business Analysis
   * Roles
   * Approaches
   * Organizational factors
   * Requirements vs. designs
* Context - How environment, constraints and ecosystems shape BA work
Case Study
* Determine the most appropriate BA approach for a new project scenario
* Assess how organizational context affects the approach
Session 3 - Change & Needs
Topics
* Change
   * Understanding change drivers
   * Assessing impact
   * Preparing for transition
* Need
   * Identifying business needs
   * Root-cause analysis
   * Problem definition
Case Study
* Perform a current-state assessment
* Identify and articulate the true business need vs. stakeholder wants
Session 4 - Requirements, Solutions & Stakeholders
Topics
* Solution
   * Defining solution options
   * Assessing potential value
* Stakeholder
   * Identifying, analyzing and engaging stakeholders
Case Study
* Create a stakeholder map
* Develop solution options and evaluate them using value, feasibility and risk criteria
Session 5 - Value & Exam Readiness
Topics
* Value
   * Assessing outcomes
   * Measuring value
   * Recommending actions to increase solution value
* Integrated review of all nine domains based on sample ECBA exam scenario questions
Case Study
* Post-implementation scenario: Evaluate whether a delivered solution achieved intended outcomes
* Recommend improvements based on value assessment
