## Overview

This is the **bi-weekly, A-session-only** format. Five Session A meetings run every two weeks, completing in approximately **10 weeks**. No Session B sessions are scheduled. No SME speakers are required.

Because Session B's real-world friction is removed, this plan extends each session's homework with a challenge task that approximates the friction participants would have received. Facilitators should also apply tighter share-out probing than in the base series.

## Extended Homework Assignments (v3 Replacements for Session B)

Each cycle's homework adds a **challenge task** that stands in for the Session B friction. These replace the Session B stretch questions. The MCQ count remains 6 per cycle.

### After Session 1A
**Standard:** Refine problem statement + one-paragraph value statement. Complete 6 MCQs.

**Challenge task (replaces Session 1B):** A leadership stakeholder has read your problem statement and responded: *"That's already what we told you — it's the booking platform. Why are we redoing the diagnosis?"* Write a 2–3 sentence response using BACCM language that explains the value of a BA-led analysis before a solution is chosen. Be specific; do not use generic language about "due diligence."

---

### After Session 2A
**Standard:** One-page BA approach recommendation (approach, stakeholders, rationale). Complete 6 MCQs.

**Challenge task (replaces Session 2B):** Your interview with the Head of Operations produced these exact notes: *"Guides don't always show up. We have a spreadsheet. It's not great."* That is the full transcript. Write three elicitation follow-up questions you would ask at a second interview — each question should target a different type of information gap (factual, motivational, and constraint-based). Explain in one sentence why each question type matters for your BA approach.

---

### After Session 3A
**Standard:** 3 raw needs converted to written requirements. Complete 6 MCQs.

**Challenge task (replaces Session 3B):** Review your three written requirements. For each one, answer the following: *"How would you verify this requirement is met during acceptance testing?"* If you cannot answer that question for a requirement, revise the requirement until you can. Show the before and after.

---

### After Session 4A
**Standard:** One-paragraph solution recommendation + RTM stub (3 requirements traced). Complete 6 MCQs.

**Challenge task (replaces Session 4B):** The CEO has responded to your recommendation: *"We looked at Option 2 six months ago and it didn't work. Why is this different?"* Write a 3–5 sentence response. Do not dismiss the CEO's concern. Acknowledge the prior attempt, identify at least one specific way the current analysis differs (use your RTM to support this), and state what you would do to address the remaining doubt before proceeding.

---

### After Session 5A
**No homework.** Review all previous MCQs. Prepare a 2-minute verbal response to: *"Looking back at the full TrailBlaze engagement, what is the one decision you would change and why?"* You will be asked this in the session 5A debrief — not as a written deliverable, but as a spoken reflection.

## Facilitator Adjustments for Removed Session B Content

### Share-out probing (Sessions 2A–5A)
In v1 and v2, Session B surfaces assumptions and weak reasoning before the next Session A. In v3, the facilitator must do this during share-outs.

**Use these probing questions during share-outs:**

| Session A | Probing questions to use during share-out |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| 2A | "If the Head of Operations gives you two conflicting answers in the same interview, how do you document that?" / "Who isn't in your stakeholder map that probably should be?" |
| 3A | "Which of your requirements could a stakeholder sign off on without understanding?" / "What would you cut if you had to scope down by 30%?" |
| 4A | "If your recommended option fails at pilot, which requirement in your RTM was the first thing you would look at?" / "How does your recommendation change if the technology budget shrinks by half?" |
| 5A | "What did the BA scoped but not prioritize — and what did that cost?" / "Which ECBA concept mattered most in this engagement and why?" |

### Optional checkpoints
The base plan includes 2 checkpoint questions per session (mid-concept). In v3, add a third checkpoint if the group completes exercises quickly or appears confident — fast completion is often a signal of under-challenged reasoning, not mastery.

Suggested third checkpoints by session:
- **1A:** "If the CEO asks you to start designing the app tomorrow, what do you say and why?"
- **2A:** "What is the difference between a BA approach and an elicitation plan?"
- **3A:** "Give me an example of a raw need that could produce three different written requirements depending on how it is scoped."
- **4A:** "What is the difference between validating a requirement and verifying it?"
- **5A:** "What does BA value mean to the person who signed off on the pilot budget?"
