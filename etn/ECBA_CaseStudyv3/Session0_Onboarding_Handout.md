# ECBA Case Study — Session 0 Onboarding Handout (v3: Bi-Weekly, Without Session B)

*Distribute before Session 1A. No meeting required.*

---

## What This Series Is

This is a 5-session, 10-week ECBA exam preparation series built around one evolving case study: **TrailBlaze Adventures**, a mid-sized adventure travel company working through a BA engagement from first contact through pilot results.

One session every two weeks. Each session is 90 minutes and covers a set of ECBA exam domains and corresponding BABOK knowledge areas. You will build real BA artifacts across all five sessions and apply them to live case data released progressively.

## What You Need to Do Between Sessions

### Reading
Each session requires a BABOK chapter set read in 2 weeks — approximately 30–50 pages per cycle.

| Session | Read Before It |
|---------|---------------|
| 1A | BABOK Ch. 1 + Ch. 2 §2.1–2.4 |
| 2A | BABOK Ch. 3 + Ch. 4 |
| 3A | BABOK Ch. 6 + Ch. 8 |
| 4A | BABOK Ch. 7 + Ch. 9 |
| 5A | BABOK Ch. 10 + all personal notes from Sessions 1–4 |

**Suggested approach:** Two reading sessions per week, 30–45 minutes each. First session: read and take Cornell notes. Second session: close the book and write a one-paragraph summary from memory.

### Homework
After each session: one short written artifact, one challenge question, and 6 ECBA exam-style MCQs. Total time: approximately 60–90 minutes.

The challenge question is the most important part. It presents a situation where your in-session work gets pushed back or tested — the kind of friction you would get from a real stakeholder. Come prepared to discuss your answer at the next session's share-out.

---

## How the Case Study Works

TrailBlaze Adventures is revealed in stages. You will not see the full picture until Session 5. Your Session 1 decisions affect your Session 3 work. A problem statement without measurable criteria leaves you with nothing to trace when the requirements come due. Build with that in mind.

---

## Active Learning Environment

Every session includes group exercises, share-outs, and timed multiple choice practice. Come prepared to work. Passive attendance does not build the pattern recognition the ECBA exam tests.
